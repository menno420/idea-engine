## The folk belief
Retries are free insurance. A task failed — a lane timed out, a call 500'd — so try it again; the more persistently you retry, the higher your success rate and the lower your visible error rate. If the fleet is a little overloaded, retries just smooth over the transient. "Never let a task die on the first failure."

## The mechanism (reasoned to its fuller form — Q-0254 duty)
Model the fleet as a pool of c=100 parallel lanes serving tasks. New tasks arrive at rate λ (tasks/tick), comfortably below capacity. A task attempt fails (times out) with a probability p that rises with the *effective* load — the more work in flight, the longer the waits, the more timeouts. Model that as a logistic in utilization:

  p(x) = p0 + (1 − p0) / (1 + exp(−k·(x/c − θ)))

with a small floor p0 at light load and p→1 once x exceeds the overload knee θ·c. Now the retry policy: a failed attempt is re-queued with probability r. So the *effective* attempt rate x is not λ — it is λ plus the retries:

  x = λ + r·x·p(x)          (self-consistency;  G(x) ≡ λ + r·x·p(x))

The fixed points of G are the fleet's steady states. This one equation carries the whole story:

1. **Two stable states, not one.** At light effective load p≈p0 is tiny, so retries add almost nothing and G crosses the identity line at a *healthy* fixed point just above λ. But G also has a *collapsed* fixed point: when x is large, p→1, so G(x)→λ+r·x, whose fixed point is x*=λ/(1−r) — a huge backlog sustained almost entirely by retries of work that keeps timing out. Between them sits an unstable fixed point: a tipping threshold. The pool is **bistable** — the SAME λ admits both a healthy and a collapsed equilibrium.

2. **The collapse is one-way (hysteresis / metastability).** Ramp λ up and the healthy branch survives until it collides with the tipping point and jumps to collapse at some λ_up. Ramp λ back down and the collapsed branch does NOT recover at λ_up — it clings on until a much lower λ_down, because the retry storm manufactures its own load. The gap λ_up − λ_down is a hysteresis loop: a brief spike above λ_up leaves the fleet stuck collapsed at loads it handled fine minutes earlier. This is a *metastable* failure — the trigger is gone but the failure state persists.

3. **Retries are the cause, not the cure (the counterintuitive dose-response).** The collapsed fixed point only exists when r is large enough. Below a critical r_c the map G has a single (healthy) fixed point — no bistability, no storm possible. Above r_c the metastable region opens and *widens monotonically with r*: the more aggressively you retry, the larger the basin of load blips that can tip you, and the deeper the collapse. The knob you reach for to raise reliability is exactly the knob that manufactures the catastrophe — and it is invisible in the steady-state error rate, which looks fine right up to the tip.

4. **The fix is a budget, not capacity.** Adding lanes (raising c) just rescales the same bistable picture. What removes the collapsed fixed point is *capping the retry load*: allow retries to inject at most b·c extra attempts/tick (a retry budget / circuit breaker). With the cap, the effective map is G_cap(x)=λ+min(r·x·p(x), b·c); its runaway branch is severed, x can never climb past λ+b·c, and if that ceiling sits below the tipping point the collapsed equilibrium simply does not exist. Reliability is bought by *limiting* retries, not by adding them or adding hardware.
