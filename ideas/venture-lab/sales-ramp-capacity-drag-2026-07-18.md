## The phenomenon (one line)
Reps ramp: a rep of tenure a books q(a)=Q·min(a/R,1), reaching full quota Q only after R months. A force growing at rate g and losing reps at attrition d has active-rep tenure distributed Exp(g+d), so the fraction of nominal quota it actually books is φ(β)=(1−e^{−βR})/(βR), β=g+d — always below 1 and falling as β rises. So realized bookings = (headcount)·φ·Q, the nameplate (headcount)·Q overstates them by 1/φ, and since φ decreases in g the overstatement grows the harder you scale hiring. The kicker: φ depends only on β=g+d, so raising growth by Δ and raising attrition by Δ produce exactly the same productivity drag — you cannot tell an over-hiring problem from a retention problem by looking at bookings-per-rep.

## The folk belief
"Bookings-per-rep is a talent/coaching signal — if it drops, our reps are underperforming, so we hire and manage harder." And separately: "Hiring ahead of the number is free upside — more reps is more capacity." Leaders read a falling per-rep number as an execution problem and respond by pouring in MORE hiring (raising g), which puts an even larger share of the force into ramp and drives per-rep productivity DOWN further — the opposite of the intended fix. They also treat a growth problem and an attrition problem as separate diagnoses, when in realized capacity they are the identical β=g+d lever.

## The mechanism (reasoned to its fuller form — Q-0254 duty)
A rep's output ramps with tenure. Model the ramp as linear to full quota Q over a ramp period R:

  q(a) = Q · min(a/R, 1),

zero at hire, rising linearly, full at tenure a=R, flat after. Now the age structure of the ACTIVE force. In a firm hiring so that headcount grows at monthly rate g, the cohort hired a months ago numbered proportional to e^{g(t−a)}; of those, the survivors present today are a fraction e^{−d·a} under memoryless attrition rate d. So the density of tenure a among currently-active reps is proportional to e^{g(t−a)}·e^{−d·a} ∝ e^{−(g+d)a}, i.e. **active tenure ~ Exp(β), β = g + d** (the Lotka stable-population age structure). The realized fraction of nominal quota the force books is the ramp averaged over that distribution:

  φ(β) = E[min(a/R, 1)],  a ~ Exp(β)
       = ∫₀^R (a/R)·β e^{−βa} da + ∫_R^∞ β e^{−βa} da
       = (1 − e^{−βR}) / (βR).

This is the classic (1−e^{−x})/x utilization form: φ(β) ∈ (0,1), φ→1 as βR→0 (a static or instantly-ramping force books full quota), and φ DECREASES monotonically as β rises. Consequences the "hire harder" instinct misses:

1. **Nameplate overstates realized capacity by 1/φ.** Realized bookings = headcount·φ·Q, not headcount·Q. Planning on nominal quota over-forecasts bookings by the factor 1/φ, and since φ falls as g rises, the over-forecast is WORST exactly when you are scaling hardest.

2. **Faster growth dilutes per-rep productivity.** ∂φ/∂g < 0: hiring ahead of the number puts more of the force in ramp, so bookings-per-rep falls as a mechanical consequence of growth, not of talent. Responding with still-more hiring makes it worse.

3. **Growth and attrition are INTERCHANGEABLE.** φ depends only on β=g+d. A +Δ increase in hiring growth and a +Δ increase in attrition produce the IDENTICAL φ, hence the identical capacity drag — and are indistinguishable from the aggregate bookings-per-rep number. So a "productivity problem" could be an over-hiring problem or a retention problem, and the aggregate cannot tell them apart; equivalently, cutting attrition by Δ is exactly as good for realized capacity as slowing over-hiring by Δ.

On the pinned world (Q=1, R=6 months, δ=0.03/month) realized productivity is φ(β_slow=0.04)=0.889 of quota at slow growth g=0.01 but φ(β_fast=0.11)=0.732 at fast growth g=0.08 — a 15.7-point drag purely from scaling hiring; and a matched attrition bump (δ: 0.03→0.10, holding g=0.01, so β=0.11) drags to the IDENTICAL 0.732, demonstrating the interchangeability.

## Why it matters (venture-ops)
Sales capacity is the core lever of a go-to-market plan, and it is routinely planned on nameplate — headcount times quota — as if a rep books full quota from day one. This proposal gives the operator a hard capacity identity: realized bookings = headcount · φ · Q with φ=(1−e^{−βR})/(βR), β = hiring-growth + attrition. Concretely here (R=6 months, δ=0.03): slow growth realizes 88.9% of quota per rep, but scaling to g=0.08 drops that to 73.2% — the last chunk of "capacity" you paid for is ramp, not bookings, and the gap widens the harder you push. Decision rules: (1) forecast on realized φ·nominal, not nominal, and re-derive φ every time you change the hiring plan (higher g ⇒ lower φ); (2) treat a falling bookings-per-rep number as ambiguous — it is β=g+d, so before "coach harder" rule out over-hiring and attrition, because the aggregate cannot distinguish them; (3) since attrition and growth enter φ identically, price a 1-point attrition reduction as exactly equal to a 1-point slowdown in over-hiring, and (4) shrink the ramp R (better onboarding, warm territories, faster pipeline) — R is the one lever that lifts φ at every β. The trap is reading a mechanical ramp-dilution of capacity as a talent problem and responding with the very hiring that deepens it.

## Model basis (declared model-dependence — the P024 discipline)
The capacity identity (realized = nominal · E[ramp over the active-tenure distribution]) and the interchangeability (any φ that averages a tenure-only ramp over the Exp(g+d) age structure depends on g and d only through β=g+d) are model-FREE: they follow from ANY per-rep ramp q(a) that depends on tenure alone, plus the stable-population age structure of a pool with inflow growth g and memoryless outflow d. What is model-dependent is the SHAPE of the ramp and the memorylessness of attrition (hence the exact φ): the pinned world uses a LINEAR ramp to full quota over R and a constant-hazard (exponential) attrition, giving the clean φ=(1−e^{−βR})/(βR). A different ramp shape (S-curve, concave, stepped) or a non-memoryless attrition (tenure-dependent hazard) changes the exact φ but not the qualitative facts (φ<1, φ decreasing in total turnover, growth and attrition entering through a common turnover rate for memoryless attrition) whenever output ramps with tenure. Assumptions: (a) productivity depends on tenure only (no cohort-quality or macro effects — a lower-quality later cohort makes the drag worse, so the claim is conservative); (b) attrition is memoryless at rate d (early-tenure "ramp-and-leave" churn concentrates leavers among the un-ramped and DEEPENS the drag, so exponential attrition is again conservative); (c) hiring grows at a constant rate g in steady state (a hiring surge transiently worsens φ beyond the steady value); (d) full quota Q is tenure-invariant after ramp. The claim is scoped: under a tenure-only ramp and constant-hazard attrition in a steadily growing force — the standard sales-capacity description — realized capacity is nominal·(1−e^{−βR})/(βR) with β=g+d, demonstrated on the pinned constants and mechanism-explained via the ramp + Lotka age structure, not asserted as a universal law. The direction (φ<1, decreasing in β, growth↔attrition interchangeable) holds for any tenure-only ramp with memoryless attrition; the pinned Q/R/δ/g are illustrative and bracket the drag.
