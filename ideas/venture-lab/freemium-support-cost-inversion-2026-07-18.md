## The phenomenon (one line)
Acquire free users at a support/infra cost c each; a fraction converts to paid worth V. The marginal free user's contribution is V·p(i)−c, where p(i) is that user's conversion probability. Because the highest-intent users are acquired first, p(i) DECLINES with volume, so marginal contribution decreases and crosses zero at n* where p(n*)=c/V. Total contribution C(n)=Σ(V·1{convert_i}−c) is therefore CONCAVE: it rises to a peak at n*, then falls, and eventually goes NEGATIVE once the accumulated serving cost of low-intent users outweighs their thin conversion value. Optimizing signups (acquire N_MAX ≫ n*) drives a program worth +C(n*) down through zero into value destruction — while signups, total conversions, and gross new logos all keep going up.

## The folk belief
"Free is a growth flywheel — more free users is always more upside, because free users cost us almost nothing and some fraction always converts." Growth teams target signups and total conversions (both monotonically increasing in acquisition), treat the free tier as a near-costless top-of-funnel, and rarely price the per-user support/infra load against the DECLINING conversion of each marginal cohort. So they scale free acquisition past the contribution optimum, then past the zero-crossing, congratulating themselves on record signups while the free program quietly turns net-negative.

## The mechanism (reasoned to its fuller form — Q-0254 duty)
Each free user carries a fixed serving cost c (support tickets, infra, storage, moderation) and converts to paid worth V with probability p. The per-free-user gross contribution is V·p − c. Acquire users in intent order (highest-intent first — they sought the product out; each later cohort is colder), so the i-th acquired user converts with probability p(i) that DECREASES in i. Model the decline as p(i)=P0·e^{−K·i} (top-intent P0, decay rate K). The marginal contribution of the i-th free user is

  marginal(i) = V·P0·e^{−K·i} − c,

DECREASING in i, positive while p(i) > c/V and negative after. It crosses zero at

  n* = ln(V·P0 / c) / K.

Total contribution is C(n) = Σ_{i=1}^{n} (V·1{convert_i} − c), with expectation

  E[C(n)] = V·S(n) − c·n,  S(n) = Σ_{i=1}^{n} P0·e^{−K·i} = P0·(1−e^{−K·n})/(e^{K}−1).

Because marginal(i) is positive then negative, E[C(n)] is CONCAVE and MAXIMIZED at n=⌊n*⌋: adding free users past n* has NEGATIVE marginal contribution, so the total falls. And it does not merely plateau — the conversion value V·S(n) SATURATES (S(n)→P0/(e^{K}−1) as the intent pool exhausts) while the serving cost c·n grows without bound, so C(n) crosses zero at some n_zero>n* and heads to −∞. Counterintuitive core: whether the free program creates value is NOT "are signups/conversions up" — they almost always are — it is whether the MARGINAL cohort's conversion still clears the serving-cost breakeven p≥c/V. On the pinned world (P0=0.10, K=0.001, V=100, c=2) the optimum is n*≈1609 with C(n*)≈+4777, but the signup-maximizing volume N_MAX=6000 has C≈−2030 — pushing from the optimum to "as many free users as we can get" flips a +4777 program into a −2030 one, a swing of ~6800 in contribution, and the whole free program is net-negative past n_zero≈4960.

## Why it matters (venture-ops)
Freemium / product-led growth is the default venture GTM, and its free tier is routinely run as a near-costless flywheel judged by signups and total conversions — the two metrics that rise with acquisition even as the free program turns net-negative. This proposal gives the operator a hard pre-commit rule: per-free-user contribution is V·p−c, so admit a marginal free cohort only while its EXPECTED conversion clears the serving-cost breakeven p ≥ c/V, and cap paid free-tier acquisition at the contribution optimum n* = ln(V·P0/c)/K. Concretely here (V=100, c=2, breakeven p=0.02): the free program peaks at ~1609 users worth +4777, is worth nothing by ~4960, and destroys ~2030 by 6000 — so the last two-thirds of "growth" is pure value destruction. Decision rule: (1) meter free acquisition against the contribution optimum, not against signups; (2) since the lever is the DECLINE in intent, spend budget on intent QUALITY (targeting, qualification, activation, paywall placement) that lifts p(i) on the margin, not on raw signup volume; (3) instrument marginal-cohort conversion (not blended average) and stop free acquisition when it drops below c/V. The trap is that conversion value saturates while serving cost is linear, so the free program that "still converts fine on average" is exactly the one bleeding value on its marginal cohort.

Here there is no cohort, no attrition, no observed-rate artifact; the intent decline is a KNOWN acquisition-order property and the result is a real contribution inversion (value actually destroyed), not a measurement mirage.
- **P098 (referral-bonus value trap):** an interior optimum on a spend lever driven by a branching referral amplifier. Here there is no branching and no referral; the concavity comes from a monotone DECLINING conversion curve against a fixed serving cost, and the "past the optimum" region is value-DESTROYING (contribution goes negative), not merely sub-optimal.
The novel content is the freemium support-cost inversion: per-free-user contribution V·p−c with p(n) declining makes total contribution V·Σp−c·n concave (peak at p=c/V, i.e. n*=ln(V·P0/c)/K) and eventually NEGATIVE because conversion value saturates while serving cost is linear — with a flat-top-intent necessity control proving the inversion is the declining conversion, not serving cost per se.

## Model basis (declared model-dependence — the P024 discipline)
The contribution identity per free user (V·p − c) and the concavity/inversion of the cumulative total whenever p(i) is decreasing are model-FREE: they follow from a fixed serving cost, a conversion value, and any DECREASING intent curve — the total is concave and, because bounded conversion value meets linear cost, eventually negative. What is model-dependent is the SHAPE of the decline (hence the exact n* and n_zero): the pinned world uses exponential intent decay p(i)=P0·e^{−K·i}, giving the clean optimum n*=ln(V·P0/c)/K and saturating conversion S(n)=P0(1−e^{−Kn})/(e^{K}−1). A different intent curve (power-law, logistic, kinked) moves n* and n_zero but not the qualitative inversion (concave, peaks at p=c/V, eventually negative) whenever intent is decreasing. Assumptions: (a) serving cost c is fixed per free user (no economies of scale in support — scale economies push the optimum out but do not remove it while c>0); (b) users are acquired in intent order so p(i) is monotone decreasing (partial mixing blunts but does not remove the concavity); (c) conversion value V is constant across cohorts (lower-intent users converting to lower-value plans makes the inversion worse, so the claim is conservative); (d) no free-user network/virality externality that raises paid conversion of others (a positive externality would offset — scoped out and flagged). The claim is scoped: under a fixed per-user serving cost and a decreasing conversion-intent acquisition curve — the standard freemium description — cumulative contribution is concave and turns negative past a threshold volume, demonstrated on the pinned constants and mechanism-explained via the per-user contribution identity, not asserted as a universal law. The direction (concave, peak at p=c/V, eventual negativity) holds for ANY decreasing intent curve; the pinned P0/K/V/c are illustrative and bracket the optimum.
