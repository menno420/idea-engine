## The phenomenon (one line)
A comp accelerator with a cliff at 100% quota makes reps rationally re-time deals across the period boundary (pull forward to clear the cliff, then pull forward more to bank at the accelerated rate), so observed bookings bunch just above quota and show a spurious negative QoQ autocorrelation — even though true demand is i.i.d. across periods and total horizon demand is conserved.

## The folk belief
"A sales-comp cliff at 100% quota just sharpens focus on hitting quota. It changes how reps are PAID, not what or when they sell — a deal is a deal, it lands when the customer signs. So aggregate team bookings and their quarter-to-quarter trend are unaffected by the comp plan; and the fact that most reps land right around quota just means the plan is well-calibrated." The comp cliff is treated as a pure payout rule with no effect on the timing, distribution, or apparent dynamics of bookings.

## The mechanism (reasoned to its fuller form — Q-0254 duty)
Model one rep's within-period payoff as a function of period bookings B against quota Q. Under an accelerator, commission is `c·min(B,Q) + c·a·max(0, B−Q)` with base rate `c` and accelerator multiple `a>1` (often plus a lump kicker at B=Q). The marginal payout per booked dollar JUMPS from `c` to `c·a` at B=Q — a **convex kink** in the payoff. A rep who can shift the TIMING of a deal across the period boundary (book it in period t or t+1) faces different marginal rates on the two sides of that kink, so the timing that maximizes total commission is generally NOT the timing the customer's natural demand implies. Two rational relocations follow, both across the t / t+1 boundary:

- **Pull-forward to clear the cliff.** A rep who ends period t just *under* quota (bookings in `[Q(1−w), Q)`) has a block of would-be-next-period demand sitting at the base rate `c` on both sides — but pulling just enough of it into period t crosses the kink, so every dollar already booked this period that was below quota now sits under an attainment ≥ 100% and (with a kicker) the crossing itself pays a lump, and the pulled dollars can be booked at the accelerated rate. The rep pulls the *minimum* volume needed to reach Q — landing **just at/above quota**. Aggregated, this piles rep-periods into a narrow band just above the cliff: **BUNCHING**.
- **Pull-forward to bank the accelerator.** A rep who is already *comfortably over* quota (attainment ≥ `1+s`) is in the accelerator zone: every additional dollar booked *this* period pays `c·a`, whereas a dollar deferred to next period risks starting below next period's quota and paying only `c`. So it is rational to pull *more* of next period's deals FORWARD and book them at the accelerated rate now. This is **"a hot period borrows from the next"**: the over-performing period is inflated further, and the next period is depleted.

Now aggregate over N reps × T periods with TRUE per-rep-per-period demand drawn **i.i.d. across periods** — no real quarter-to-quarter dependence in the market. Three observable artifacts emerge in the OBSERVED (relocated) series that are ABSENT from the TRUE (un-relocated) counterfactual:

1. **BUNCHING.** The pull-to-clear reps land just above Q, and reps already in the `[Q, Q(1+ε)]` band stay put, so the cross-sectional attainment distribution has excess mass in the band — exactly the bunching-at-a-kink signature (Saez 2010) transplanted from the tax-kink literature to the quota kink.
2. **SPURIOUS NEGATIVE lag-1 autocorrelation.** Every pull-forward moves demand from period t+1 into period t: it raises the team total in t and lowers it in t+1. A period made hot by borrowing is followed by a depleted period, so consecutive team totals are anti-correlated — the observed series has a **negative lag-1 autocorrelation** even though the true series' autocorrelation is ≈ 0 (the paired counterfactual isolates this from the finite-sample autocorrelation bias, which both worlds share).
3. **CONSERVATION.** Relocation only MOVES deals between existing adjacent periods; it never creates or destroys demand. So the total bookings over the horizon are exactly conserved: `observed_total = true_total`. The QoQ volatility and mean-reversion a forecaster reads off the observed series is therefore a pure **artifact of the comp plan**, not a property of the market — the relocation redistributes a fixed pie across the period axis.

The trap for the naive analyst: reading the OBSERVED series, they conclude "our demand is volatile and mean-reverting" (from the spurious negative autocorrelation) and "the plan is well-calibrated — most reps land right at quota" (from the bunching), when BOTH are cliff artifacts on an i.i.d., conserved true-demand process.

## Why it matters (venture-ops)
Sales forecasting, board commits, quota-setting, and hiring/spend plans lean on the observed bookings series and the observed attainment distribution. This result says both are DISTORTED by the comp cliff in opposite-but-compounding ways: (i) the attainment distribution BUNCHES just above quota, so "most reps land right at quota" reads as a well-calibrated plan when it is actually reps re-timing deals to clear the cliff — the true demand distribution is smooth and much of the band mass is borrowed from adjacent periods; (ii) the team-total series acquires a spurious NEGATIVE QoQ autocorrelation, so a forecaster fitting an AR/mean-reversion model onto observed bookings will "discover" volatility and mean-reversion that are pure comp-plan timing, over-provisioning for a market swing that isn't there. The fix is to treat attainment bunching and QoQ negative autocorrelation as comp-plan-timing SIGNATURES, not market signals: de-bias the forecast off a horizon-aggregate the relocation conserves (the total is invariant), estimate demand on a coarser time grid than the comp period, and stress-test whether an "improvement" in QoQ smoothness or quota-attainment came from the market or from the plan's kink. It also reframes plan design: a sharper cliff (bigger accelerator multiple, larger kicker) buys MORE bunching and MORE spurious autocorrelation — cleaner-looking attainment, noisier-looking demand — without changing a single unit of true demand.

## Model basis (declared model-dependence — the P024 discipline)
- **Pure above-quota ACCELERATOR (convex kink), no ratchet.** The pinned world models a comp plan whose only kink is the accelerator at quota (base rate below, accelerated rate above). Under this plan the dominant above-cliff re-timing is to PULL FORWARD and bank at the accelerated rate, which is WHY the observed lag-1 autocorrelation is negative ("a hot period borrows from the next"). This is the load-bearing modelling choice, declared not hidden: under a QUOTA-RATCHET plan (next period's quota set from this period's attainment) an over-quota rep faces the opposite marginal incentive — defer/sandbag to avoid ratcheting their own target — which pushes the autocorrelation toward positive/smoothing. The pinned world commits to ρ=0 (no ratchet); the ratchet axis is named as the distinct next slice (see the session card's 💡). The BUNCHING and CONSERVATION artifacts are robust to the ratchet sign; only the autocorrelation sign is regime-dependent.
- **Relocation is across ADJACENT periods only, and only between existing periods.** Deals move between t and t+1 (not across multiple boundaries in one step), and no deal leaks past the last period — this is what makes horizon demand exactly conserved. Allowing multi-period relocation would only strengthen the timing effects; capping at adjacent periods is the conservative, cleanly-conserved choice.
- **TRUE demand i.i.d. across periods.** The counterfactual has no real quarter-to-quarter dependence, so the ENTIRE observed autocorrelation and QoQ volatility is attributable to relocation. Real demand with genuine seasonality/trend would ADD its own autocorrelation on top; the i.i.d. pinning is what lets G2's paired difference attribute the negative autocorrelation to the comp plan alone.
- **Robustness across the demand distribution.** G3(b) re-runs the whole battery under a second generative process (Poisson deal COUNT with unit amounts, vs Exponential deal AMOUNTS) and the bunching and negative-autocorrelation effects persist ≥3σ, so the result is not an artifact of the exponential-amount choice. The concrete magnitudes (band excess ≈ 74 rep-periods, autocorr diff ≈ −0.15) are specific to the pinned constants; the SIGNS and the "artifact-not-real, conserved" conclusion are general to any convex-kink comp plan with re-timable deals.

## Probe report (v0, self-adversarial)
**1. You describe reps over quota "sandbagging" (pushing deals BACK) in the folk framing, but the verifier has them PULL FORWARD — which is it?** The verifier commits to a pure above-quota ACCELERATOR, and under that plan the rational above-cliff move is to pull forward and bank at the accelerated rate — that IS "a hot period borrows from the next," which is what produces the pre-registered NEGATIVE autocorrelation. Genuine sandbagging (defer to next period) is the OPPOSITE-sign re-timing and arises under quota RATCHETING, a different plan feature the pinned world excludes (ρ=0). The Model basis declares this explicitly, and the ratchet-coefficient sweep — where the autocorrelation SIGN flips at a ≥3σ-resolved ρ\* — is named as the distinct next venture slice. G2 is the accelerator's signature; the model is self-consistent with the gate it tests, not with a hand-wave.

**2. Isn't the negative autocorrelation just the small-sample bias of the sample autocorrelation (≈ −1/(P−1) ≈ −0.14 for P=8)?** No — that bias is exactly why the gate is PAIRED. Both the observed and the true series are length-8 and carry the SAME −0.14-ish finite-sample bias; the true world's autocorr is −0.129676, right at the bias. G2 tests the DIFFERENCE observed − true = −0.145509, which nets out the shared bias and isolates the relocation's incremental negativity (observed −0.275 is nearly DOUBLE the bias). The z is on that paired difference.

**3. Is the bunching just because you defined the band around the mean where mass already sits?** The band `[1.0, 1.1]` is compared observed-vs-true on the SAME band, paired per trial. The true world has 51.66 rep-periods in the band naturally; the observed world has 126.16 — the EXCESS 74.5 is manufactured by pull-to-clear reps crossing into the band, which the true world does not have. The gate is the excess, not the level.

**4. Is conservation trivially zero because you move exact float amounts?** Yes — and that is the POINT, made falsifiable. Relocation moves whole deal amounts between existing adjacent periods with no leak past the horizon, so `observed_total = true_total` to floating-point (conservation_max_rel = 0.0 ≤ 1e-6 across all 800 trials, both distributions). G3(a) turns "the effect is timing not magnitude" into a checked invariant: if any deal were created or destroyed, or leaked past the last period, the tolerance would catch it. A zero here is a proof, not a tautology.

**5. Does the result survive a different true-demand distribution, or is it an exponential-tail artifact?** It survives. G3(b) re-runs under Poisson deal COUNTS with unit amounts (a different generative mechanism — count randomness, not amount randomness): band_z = +175.223184 and ac_z = −13.109365, both comfortably ≥3σ, same signs. The signs and the conservation conclusion are distribution-general.

**6. Would coarser or finer deal granularity change the story?** Granularity sets the tightness of the bunching spike, not its existence. Smaller deals let pull-to-clear reps land in a NARROWER band above quota (less overshoot), sharpening the spike; larger deals widen it. The verifier pulls smallest-first to minimize overshoot, matching the rational "clear the cliff with minimal pulled volume" behavior; with mean deal ≈ 0.083·Q the overshoot is well inside the 10% band. The bunching and the negative autocorrelation are robust to granularity; only the band width is sensitive.

**7. Why measure autocorrelation on TEAM totals rather than per-rep series?** Because the folk belief and the forecaster's error live at the AGGREGATE: a RevOps analyst reads team/segment bookings QoQ, not individual reps. Per-rep relocation is idiosyncratic, but it aggregates into a team-total series whose lag-1 autocorrelation is the object a forecaster actually fits. The gate targets exactly the series the trap fools.

**8. Could the negative autocorrelation cascade away (every period borrows, so all periods end near quota with no alternation)?** That failure mode is exactly what the OVER_THRESH banking behavior avoids: over-quota reps AMPLIFY hot periods by pulling MORE forward (not merely clearing to quota), so hot periods stay hot and their successors are depleted — the alternation survives rather than washing to a flat quota line. The measured observed autocorr (−0.275, nearly double the true bias) confirms the alternation is real and strong, not a cascaded flat line (which would push autocorr toward the bias, not away from it).
