## What it is

An e-book distilled from this fleet's real, running doctrine: section claims, coordinator heartbeats, the merge-on-green ceremony, the probe battery, and owner-gate discipline — told as the operational story of an actual autonomous agent fleet, not a hypothetical framework. The raw material is already written across fleet-manager, superbot, and kit docs; the book is a distillation and narration pass, not research. Sell channel: Gumroad e-book at $15–29, with excerpt chapters published free on the websites lane for reach.

## Why shortlisted

The material is unique: first-hand operational doctrine from a fleet that actually runs, which very few others have in 2026 — that is a moat no amount of competitor writing effort erases quickly. Drafting is nearly free because the doctrine already exists in written form; the fleet's cost is assembly and polish. And it has the strongest synergy on the list: it is the middle of the distribution stack the batch's cross-cutting note identifies — the starter kit (rank 1) is the product the book explains, the devlog (batch #12) is the audience channel the book feeds and feeds from.

## Smallest shippable slice

An outline plus one polished chapter — the claims/merge-on-green ceremony chapter — published free on the websites lane to test pull. Free-chapter engagement is the cheapest possible demand signal before any further drafting is spent.

## Honest effort / plausibility

Drafting is fleet-cheap, but two constraints are binding. First, the owner's voice/edit pass is essential — an e-book that reads as agent-assembled doctrine dumps loses exactly the authenticity that makes the material valuable, so owner time is a hard input, not a nice-to-have. Second, marketing reach caps everything: per Q-0259 r.4, the conservative expectation is low tens of sales without an audience, which is why the free-excerpt channel and the devlog matter as much as the book itself.

## Open questions

- Owner appetite: is the voice/edit pass something the owner actually wants to spend hours on? Without it the project should not start.
- Free-chapter choice: is the claims/merge-on-green ceremony the right hook, or does a war-story chapter (an incident and its fix) pull harder as the free excerpt?
