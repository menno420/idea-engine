## Problem

product-forge ORDER 001's `why:` line records an owner decision that currently lives
nowhere else: *"the full concept heads to sim-lab for an evidence pass later ('could
be simulated later' — owner), so phase 1 stays mock-data-first and cheap to
redirect."* The deferral is deliberate and correct — but as ORDER prose it is an
orphan-risk head: no idea file, no proposal, nothing that ever comes due. This repo's
mandate is that ideas end evidence-checked, parked, or rejected — never orphaned —
and routing sim-ready questions to sim-lab is exactly its outbox surface (Q-0264).

## Idea

Hold the deferral here as a probeable head. When probed, shape the **ONE** simulator
question for the full games-web concept — a Shakes & Fidget-style comic browser-RPG
presentation over superbot's existing games — and route a PROPOSAL via
`control/outbox.md`. The scope-seam capture sharpens what that question is: with
superbot-mineverse already shipping direct utility views over the same miner state,
the concept's genuinely open question is the **value of the presentation layer
itself** (does the comic-RPG skin earn a second frontend over data players can
already see?) — which is precisely the redirect decision phase 1's mock-first
cheapness was bought to protect. The probe must also check the trivial exit: if the
seam probe ends in fold/specialize, the evidence pass may collapse into that ruling
and this head parks with it.

**Why now (and honestly, why not sooner):** not expiring — this head can wait behind
expiring ones, and it is sequenced after the seam probe by design. It is captured now
because the redirect window is what decays: the evidence pass is worth most *before*
phase-2 investment makes redirecting expensive, and the batched read-only-API ORDER
(which unblocks phase 2) is heading to the manager's routing table.
