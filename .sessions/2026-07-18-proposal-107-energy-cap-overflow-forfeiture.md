## 💡 Session idea
"Discard-on-overflow cap on a regenerating budget ⇒ usable fraction 1−e^{−C/μ}" is a reusable audit lens for any metered resource that regenerates at a fixed rate, is banked against a hard cap, and is drained at irregular intervals. Whenever the overflow past the cap is forfeited, the fraction of the generated budget a consumer actually captures is concave in the cap and decreasing in their inter-arrival gap — so raising the cap saturates fast and the forfeiture is a regressive tax on infrequent/absent consumers. The transferable audit: whenever a cap sits on top of a regenerating allowance, ask "what fraction of what we generate does an infrequent consumer actually get to use, as a function of their cadence?" — and if lifetime throughput is arrival-frequency-limited rather than generation-rate-limited, bank overflow into an uncapped reserve (or scale the cap to realized cadence) before calling the cap "generous". Candidate future game/fleet heads: rate-limit token-bucket burst-credit forfeiture, PTO/leave-accrual cap forfeiture for employees who can't take leave, retry/quota bucket overflow under bursty load, and loyalty-points expiry caps — each a "regenerating budget + discard-on-overflow cap ⇒ 1−e^{−C/μ} usable, regressive in absence" claim with the same exponential-overflow closed-form anchor and a within-cadence anchor-match control σ.
