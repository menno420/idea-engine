## 💡 Session idea

**A close-out baton that caches a DERIVED value instead of citing the rule
that derives it becomes a drift vector the moment the derivation has one
more step in it.** The EAP walkthrough's §E baton says "round 13 opens at
fleet-backlogs" — a cached OUTPUT of the rotation rule, computed one slot
early (it skipped the unserved round-12 unrelated closer). Every committed
PRIMARY source (ORDER 004 rule 3's cycle, the per-slot spacing-4 histories
restated on every drafter card) yields P064 = unrelated unambiguously, so
the error was recoverable in one minute — but only because the rule and its
history live on the cards; a reader trusting the baton alone would have
drafted the wrong slot and broken the spacing-4 invariant every future
drafter checks against. The durable pattern: batons and handoffs should
carry the RULE plus the raw state ("3 of 4 round-12 slots served; unrelated
is next per rule 3"), never a pre-computed conclusion ("round 13 opens at
X") — a cached conclusion has no expiry check, while a rule+state baton
re-derives itself correct at read time. Same family as P063's "ordering is
an unpriced constant" and P060's "record the drop REASON": write down the
generator, not the generated value.
