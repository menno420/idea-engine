# VERDICT 133 mirror — the German-tank MVUE: you observe K distinct serial numbers drawn WITHOUT replacement from an unknown fleet numbered 1..N and must estimate N. The naive estimate "N = the largest serial I have seen" (the sample maximum m) is systematically too LOW — E[m]=K(N+1)/(K+1)<N, a shortfall of (N−K)/(K+1) — because a finite sample almost never contains the very top of the range. The counterintuitive fix ADDS BACK the expected gap: the minimum-variance UNBIASED estimator EXTRAPOLATES ABOVE the largest observed serial, N̂=m(1+1/K)−1=m+(m−K)/K="sample maximum + the average gap between the ordered samples", exactly unbiased (E[N̂]=N) with exact variance Var(N̂)=(N−K)(N+1)/(K(K+2)); because m≥K always, N̂≥m always — the honest estimate is at or above the largest serial seen, never below. It is the MVUE because m is a complete sufficient statistic (Lehmann–Scheffé), and the competing unbiased mean-doubling estimator Ñ=2x̄−1 is strictly LESS efficient (Var(Ñ)/Var(N̂)=(K+2)/3≈2.33× noisier at K=5) because m captures all the sample's information about N — so the best estimate of a population size is LARGER than the largest one you have seen, and the max-based correction is both correct and minimum-variance. ## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the German-tank MVUE as a STATIC-POPULATION estimation law — a FIXED 1..N with uniform, gap-free numbering sampled WITHOUT replacement — and proves the max-based gap correction is unbiased and minimum-variance. But the two assumptions that make N̂ clean are exactly the ones a real ID space violates, and each violation moves the realized estimate in a direction the static MVUE cannot see. A genuinely different NEXT unrelated slice, distinct from this static-N head and from the prior UNRELATED closers (P100 Kelly, P104 epidemic overshoot, P108 regression-to-mean, P112 series MTBF, P116 arcsine), pins a GROWING-POPULATION / LENGTH-BIASED mechanism: the **live-serial-range length-bias trap** — when the ID space is monotonically GROWING and you sample K live IDs at a random wall-clock instant (K request/invoice/session IDs from a stream whose max is still climbing), the sample is length-biased toward the current high-water, so the naive max is biased HIGH relative to the true issued-count-at-sample-time while the static MVUE gap-correction OVER-shoots (a sign-flip of P120's static under-estimate). Seed-pinnable head: model a Poisson-in-time ID issuance whose max grows, sample K live IDs at a random instant, and pre-register (G-a) a length-bias gate — the growing-stream sampler's realized N̂ OVER-estimates the true count-at-instant by a floor at ≥3σ (inverting P120's under-estimate direction); (G-b) a static control — freezing the population to a fixed 1..N recovers P120's unbiased MVUE within 3σ, isolating the GROWTH (not the estimator) as the cause of the over-shoot; and (G-c) a closed-form anchor — the growing-stream bias matches the length-biased-sampling analytic correction within 3σ, so the operator lesson sharpens from "add the average gap to the sample max" to "check whether the label space is static or growing before applying the gap correction — a live, still-climbing ID space is length-biased and needs the OPPOSITE-sign adjustment." A seed-pinnable estimation-theory mechanism deliberately distinct from this slice's static-population head and from the prior unrelated closers.
