## What happened
- Idea: a noisy repeated measure has a pre score X and a post score Y of the SAME unit with test-retest reliability rho<1 (the correlation of two noisy reads of one fixed latent ability), and NOTHING is done between the reads. Select the "underperformers" — every unit with X below a fixed cutoff c=-1.0 (more than 1 SD below the mean) — apply an inert intervention (literally nothing), and re-measure. The selected group's mean IMPROVES, strongly and reproducibly, purely from regression to the mean: their extreme-low X was partly bad luck, and luck does not repeat, so Y drifts back toward the population mean. The apparent improvement is exactly Delta* = (rho-1)*E[X|X<c], strictly positive for rho<1, c<0 — a large, hundred-sigma "effect" from an intervention that does nothing. The whole population shows zero change (no time trend); the top-tail (over-performers) shows a symmetric spurious DECLINE. ## 💡 Session idea
"Select on an extreme of a noisy measure, then re-measure ⇒ a spurious (rho−1)*E[X|selected] shift" is a reusable audit lens for any before/after comparison whose group was chosen BECAUSE it was extreme. Whenever a cohort is picked for being the worst (or best) performers and then re-measured, part of its extremeness was luck that does not repeat, so it regresses toward the mean by exactly the (1−rho) fraction — manufacturing an "improvement" (or "decline") with zero real intervention. The transferable audit: whenever an intervention is applied to a selected-because-extreme group and judged by its own before/after change, ask "what would a randomized control (or the unselected population) have done?" — and if the measure is noisy and the selection was on the measure itself, subtract the regression prediction (rho−1)*E[X|selected] before crediting the intervention. Candidate future fleet/statistics heads: "we fixed the worst-performing servers/regions/queues and they improved" (RTM, not the fix), post-hoc reward for the bottom-decile cohort, seasoning/vintage effects read as quality improvement, and any KPI dashboard that triages the extreme tail and re-scores it — each a "selection-on-extremes + noisy re-measure ⇒ spurious (rho−1) shift" claim with the same exact Galton closed-form anchor and a whole-population no-trend control σ.
