## 💡 Session idea

This slice pins the Gresham hoarding at ONE depreciation model (a per-period multiplicative melt GAMMA=0.90 on the UNSPENT bad balance) and contrasts exactly TWO spender policies (naive proportional vs fully-rational bad-first). A genuinely distinct next GAME-slot slice pins the **PARTIAL-rationality / heterogeneous-population** regime: real economies are neither all-naive nor all-rational — they are a MIX, a fraction p of hoarders and 1−p of proportional spenders, and each hoarder may be only BOUNDEDLY rational (spends bad-first only when the expected melt exceeds a switching-cost threshold). The pre-registrable structure: (G-a) confirm the pure-naive gap is negative and the pure-rational gap is positive by ≥3σ (the two poles, as here); (G-b) a monotonicity headline — sweep the hoarder fraction p from 0 to 1 and confirm the aggregate circulation gap is strictly INCREASING in p by ≥3σ, crossing zero at some p\* where the good currency's velocity advantage exactly cancels (the tipping fraction of rational agents at which Gresham's law begins to bite the aggregate); (G-c) a melt-threshold control — confirm that as GAMMA rises toward 1 (a vanishing holding penalty) the rational gap shrinks monotonically toward the naive gap, so the Gresham effect is proportional to the depreciation rate, not binary. The operator lesson sharpens from "rational hoarders drive good money out" to "the good currency's velocity is a monotone function of BOTH the fraction of rational agents AND the depreciation rate — there is a tipping fraction p\* and a melt floor below which the folk belief still holds." A seed-pinnable monetary-microeconomics mechanism deliberately distinct from this two-pole sign-flip head, converting the current all-or-nothing rationality contrast into a first-class question (how much rationality, at what depreciation rate, does it take to flip the sign?).
