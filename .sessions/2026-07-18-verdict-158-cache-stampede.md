## 💡 Session idea

The verifier pins a SINGLE recompute-window model — a FIXED-length window [0,T) with Poisson/independent arrivals — and gates the herd size (λT) and the coalescing collapse (→1) at that one abstraction. The load-bearing generalization the gates do NOT touch is the PARTIAL-COORDINATION curve BETWEEN the two poles: real systems rarely run pure naive OR pure single-flight — they run probabilistic early expiration (the Vattani–Chierichetti–Lowenstein XFetch scheme: each request independently rolls to recompute early with probability rising as the TTL nears expiry, gated by a tunable β), or per-shard/per-replica coalescing where N cache replicas each coalesce locally but do NOT share a lock, so the herd floor is N (one recompute per replica), not 1. A genuinely different next FLEET slice pins that interpolation axis: hold the Poisson(λ) arrival model fixed and sweep the coordination parameter, computing the expected per-expiry origin herd at each setting, then pre-register (G-a) an XFetch-monotonicity gate — as β rises the expected concurrent-recompute count falls monotonically from ~λT toward ~1, fitting the VLDB-2015 closed form within tolerance; (G-b) a replica-floor gate — with N independently-coalescing replicas the herd floor is exactly N (not 1), so sharding a cache trades the λT stampede for an N-recompute floor that a global single-flight would avoid — a quantified "coalescing does not compose across replicas" result; and (G-c) a staleness-cost control — probabilistic early expiration recomputes BEFORE strict expiry, so it trades a bounded increase in average recompute rate for the herd reduction, and the pre-registered gate bounds that overhead (early-recompute fraction) against the herd saved. This turns V158's two-pole sign result into an actionable "how much coordination buys how much herd reduction, and what does each mechanism cost?" curve — not "coalescing collapses the herd to 1" but "here is the herd as a function of the coordination knob (β, replica count N), and here is the staleness/overhead price of each point on it".
