## 💡 Session idea
The proof pins a TWO-segment mixture with a FIXED spread (r_A=0.97, r_B=0.55, Δr=0.42) and a FIXED 50/50 weight, and shows the observed curve rises by ≈0.21 toward max(r_A,r_B). But the mirage's SIZE is governed jointly by the between-segment hazard spread Δr=r_A−r_B and the churny share w_B: the asymptotic lift max(r)−(w_A·r_A+w_B·r_B) is product-form — it VANISHES at both w_B→0 and w_B→1 (a homogeneous base has no survivorship bias) and is maximized at an INTERIOR churny share, while increasing monotonically in Δr — so the single headline lift hides a non-monotone-in-composition, monotone-in-spread surface. A genuinely different next VENTURE slice sweeps BOTH axes over the same SEED-pinned cohort draws (w_B ∈ {0.1,0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9} at fixed Δr, then Δr ∈ {0.1,0.2,0.3,0.42} at fixed w_B) and pre-registers two ≥3σ gates: (G-a) the observed asymptotic lift traces a strictly concave-in-w_B curve whose ARGMAX matches the analytic interior maximizer within 3σ (an interior worst-case churny share, NOT the most-churny base); and (G-b) the lift is strictly INCREASING in Δr with the observed slope matching d(lift)/d(Δr) from the closed form within 3σ.
