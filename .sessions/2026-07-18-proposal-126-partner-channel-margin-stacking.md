## 💡 Session idea
"Every independent decision-maker you insert between cost and customer sets its OWN markup, and markups on a shared demand curve STACK past the joint optimum" is a reusable channel/pipeline audit lens. The integrated (single-decider) outcome maximizes total pie by construction, so any additional independent margin-setting stage — a reseller, a marketplace fee-taker, a sub-agency, an API middleman, even an internal team that prices its output to the next team — can only push the end price up and the total pie down (double marginalization). The transferable corrections: (1) count the independent margin-setting STAGES, not just their cuts — each stage is a multiplicative loss, not an additive one; (2) the cure is never "negotiate a smaller cut" but "remove the independent decision" — integrate the stage, or write a contract (two-part tariff = transfer AT COST + fixed fee; resale-price ceiling; revenue-share) that makes the downstream stage price as if it owned the full margin; (3) any place where two parties each optimize a markup on the same demand is a candidate — vertical supply chains, platform take-rates layered on seller margins, layered SaaS resell/white-label, multi-tier affiliate stacks. Candidate future venture/channel heads with the same "independent stages overshoot the joint optimum" spine: the platform-take-rate-on-seller-margin double squeeze (a marketplace fee stacked on a seller markup lifting the buyer price past what a first-party store would charge); the two-part-tariff cure quantified (a franchise-fee contract restoring the integrated price and pie); and the layered-affiliate/reseller cascade (each tier's markup compounding, price rising geometrically in the number of tiers).
