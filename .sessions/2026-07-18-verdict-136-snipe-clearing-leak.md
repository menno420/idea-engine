## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the snipe clearing leak as a STATIC per-drop-rate law — one fixed q per world, a Bernoulli(q) transmission failure independent across bidders — and proves the leak is monotone in q and vanishes at q=0. But the assumption that makes the leak clean (independent, homogeneous q) is exactly the one a live auction house violates: drop probability is CORRELATED with bidder value through connection quality, because the best-connected clients (lowest q) are disproportionately the sophisticated high-value snipers. A genuinely different NEXT game slice, distinct from this homogeneous-q head and from the prior game closers (P099 shop-reroll-ruin, P103 streak-shield, P107 energy-cap, P111 matchmaking-winrate-mirage, P115 compounding-reward-inequality, P119 prd-proc-compression, P091 gacha-pity, P095 rubber-band), pins a VALUE-CORRELATED-DROP mechanism: the **connection-quality clearing skew** — when q is negatively correlated with valuation (high-value bidders transmit MORE reliably), the hard-close leak SHRINKS in magnitude but the incidence CONCENTRATES: the value transfer flows specifically to the best-connected high-value bidder, a pay-to-connect advantage the homogeneous model averages away. Seed-pinnable head: draw (value, q) jointly with a tunable negative correlation ρ, and pre-register (G-a) an incidence gate — the fraction of the leak captured by the top connection-quality quantile exceeds its population share by ≥3σ as |ρ| grows (isolating pay-to-connect concentration); (G-b) a homogeneous-q control — setting ρ=0 recovers this slice's leak curve within 3σ (isolating the CORRELATION, not the drop rate, as the cause of the skew); (G-c) a soft-close remedy gate — the auto-extend clear removes both the leak AND its concentration within 3σ, so the operator lesson sharpens from "measure q and price the leak" to "measure the value×connection correlation before pricing the leak — a correlated drop rate turns a broad gold-sink under-drain into a targeted subsidy to your best-connected whales, and only soft-close neutralizes both."
