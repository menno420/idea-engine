## Objective

Draft and land round-32 UNRELATED-slot PROPOSAL 140 — gambler's ruin edge asymmetry. A gambler starts at `i` units, bets one fixed unit per round, wins with probability p (q=1−p), and stops the first time the fortune hits 0 (ruin) or a target N (cash out). The classical first-passage law gives P(reach N before 0) = (1−(q/p)^i)/(1−(q/p)^N) for p≠0.5, and i/N for p=0.5. The counterintuitive headline: dropping from a fair game to one with only a ~1.5% edge collapses the reach-your-target probability by ORDERS OF MAGNITUDE — the penalty is EXPONENTIAL in N (through the barrier ratio (q/p)^N), NOT linear in the edge.
