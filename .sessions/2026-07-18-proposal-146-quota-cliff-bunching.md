## 💡 Session idea

The verifier pins a pure above-quota ACCELERATOR (a convex kink: base rate below quota, accelerated rate above), under which the dominant above-cliff re-timing is to PULL FORWARD and bank at the accelerated rate — which is why the observed lag-1 autocorrelation is NEGATIVE ("a hot period borrows from the next"). The load-bearing axis the gates do NOT touch is the QUOTA-RATCHET regime: when next period's quota is set from this period's attainment (a near-universal "high water mark" plan feature), a rep comfortably over quota faces the OPPOSITE marginal incentive — defer (sandbag) marginal deals into next period to avoid ratcheting their own target — which pushes the autocorrelation the other way (toward positive / smoothing). A genuinely different next VENTURE slice pins that axis: hold the demand process fixed and sweep a ratchet coefficient ρ (next quota = base + ρ·max(0, attainment−1)·quota), then pre-register (G-a) a sign-flip gate — the observed lag-1 autocorrelation crosses from NEGATIVE (ρ=0, pure accelerator, this card) to POSITIVE (ρ high, ratchet-dominated sandbagging) at a ≥3σ-resolved ρ*; (G-b) a bunching-persistence gate — the [Q,Q(1+eps)] bunching survives across the whole ρ grid (both regimes bunch AT the cliff, only the cross-period timing sign differs); and (G-c) a conservation control — horizon demand stays conserved regardless of ρ (the artifact is always timing, never magnitude). That converts "an accelerator makes observed bookings look negatively autocorrelated" into "here is the ratchet knob ρ and the autocorrelation sign it controls" — a seed-pinnable comp-design mechanism distinct from this slice's pure-accelerator model and from every prior venture head.
