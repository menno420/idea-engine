## What happened
- Idea: a price cut of depth d on a product with gross margin m=(P0−C)/P0 sets the promo unit margin to P0(m−d) — every point of discount comes one-for-one off the unit margin because cost C is fixed — so holding gross profit constant requires a unit-volume uplift of exactly u*(d)=m/(m−d)−1=d/(m−d). This breakeven uplift is CONVEX and increasing, with a vertical asymptote at d=m: for any d≥m no finite volume breaks even (selling at/below cost). Under exponential willingness-to-pay D(P)=e^{−λP} (point elasticity λP) the realized uplift from P0 to P0(1−d) is e^{λP0d}−1 and the exact gross-profit ratio is GP(d)/GP(0)=e^{λP0d}·(m−d)/m; the discount pays only when the realized uplift factor e^{λP0d} clears the breakeven factor m/(m−d), i.e. elasticity above λ*=ln(m/(m−d))/(P0d). ## 💡 Session idea
"A discount of depth d on gross margin m needs a unit-volume uplift of exactly d/(m−d) (convex, impossible for d≥m) just to hold gross profit, and pays only when realized elasticity clears λ*=ln(m/(m−d))/(P0d)" is a reusable pricing-decision lens for any depth-based discount, coupon, or promotion. Whenever a promotion is judged by unit volume or top-line revenue — the two metrics a discount almost always improves while gross profit falls — the operator is missing that the breakeven uplift is convex and margin-coupled, so the shallow-looking discount on the thin-margin SKU is exactly the one that bleeds most. The transferable audit: compute the breakeven uplift d/(m−d) for the actual margin before running the promo, measure the SKU's price elasticity against the breakeven elasticity λ*, reserve deep cuts for SKUs that clear it, and treat "units went up" as the null expectation of any discount rather than proof it paid. Candidate future venture/pricing heads: bundle-discount breakeven where the pooled margin masks a thin-margin anchor item; loyalty/coupon stacking whose combined depth silently crosses d=m on the discounted SKU; subscription "annual discount" whose breakeven retention uplift exceeds the realized retention gain; and any "run a sale to move units" decision priced on volume instead of gross profit — each a "breakeven uplift d/(m−d), pays iff elasticity clears λ*" claim with the same convex margin-coupled anchor and an elastic-demand necessity control.
