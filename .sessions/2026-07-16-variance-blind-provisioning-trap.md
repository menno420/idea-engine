## 💡 Session idea

The variance-blind provisioning trap. Two fleet-lane M/G/1 queues, IDENTICAL arrival rate (λ=0.8), IDENTICAL mean task cost (E[S]=1.0), and therefore IDENTICAL nominal utilization ρ=0.8 — differing ONLY in service-time variance (lane A exponential CV=1, lane B heavy-tailed hyperexponential CV=3). By Pollaczek–Khinchine the mean queue wait scales with (1+CV²)/2, so lane B waits 5× longer in queue and violates its sojourn SLA 3.67× more often — purely from variance, with zero difference in ρ or mean cost. A fleet manager provisioning capacity from a backlog COUNT (i.e. from the mean) systematically under-serves the high-variance lane and never sees it coming: the counts look identical. The trap has a dose-response (the SLA gap is already 2× by a modest CV of 1.5) and a clean correction (parity is restored only by dropping the heavy-tailed lane's ρ well below A's — provision by (1+CV²), not the mean; here capacity ×1.56, ρ_B≈0.512). The transferable audit: before sizing a lane's capacity off its mean cost, measure its service-time CV and weight by (1+CV²).
