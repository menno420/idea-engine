# VERDICT 142 mirror — the correlated-vote Condorcet jury ceiling / the correlated-vote breakdown of the Condorcet Jury Theorem: intuition treats a majority vote as self-correcting — if each of N agents is better than a coin flip (p>1/2), redundancy averages out their INDEPENDENT mistakes and the majority is correct with probability → 1 (Condorcet Jury Theorem), so a bigger quorum / more monitors / more ensemble members is more reliability. But agents rarely fail independently: they share an error source (one stale upstream, one training prior, one correlated feature). Model it with a one-factor latent-Gaussian probit — agent i's latent score X_i=√ρ·C+√(1−ρ)·ε_i with C,ε_i~N(0,1) iid, vote CORRECT iff X_i>τ, τ=Φ⁻¹(1−p) so the marginal P(correct)=p for every agent regardless of ρ, pairwise latent error-correlation ρ. Condition on the common factor C: votes are independent with per-agent correct-prob q(C)=Φ((√ρ·C−τ)/√(1−ρ)); by the LLN the fraction correct→q(C), so as N→∞ the majority is correct iff q(C)>1/2 iff C>τ/√ρ — the whole jury collapses onto which side of a threshold the shared factor C landed. Hence the majority accuracy has a CLOSED-FORM CEILING A_∞=P(C>τ/√ρ)=Φ(Φ⁻¹(p)/√ρ)<1 for every ρ>0, with A_∞→1 only as ρ→0 (the Condorcet limit). Correlation does not merely SLOW the climb, it CAPS it — accuracy converges strictly below certainty, and the effective crowd saturates near N≈1/ρ. Two facts the "more voters = more accuracy" instinct misses: (1) it is CORRELATION, not competence — with ρ=0 the SAME agents (identical p) follow Condorcet and climb to ~1, the ceiling appears the instant ρ>0; (2) the effective crowd is FINITE and tiny — accuracy reaches within a hair of A_∞ by N≈1/ρ (here ≈3.3), so a 501-agent correlated jury (0.6786) is barely better than a 15-agent one and much worse than a handful of INDEPENDENT agents.
