## What happened
- Idea: estimate an unknown population size N from `K` DISTINCT serial numbers sampled without replacement from `1..N`. The naive estimate "N = the largest serial I have seen" (the sample maximum `m`) is systematically too LOW — `E[m] = K(N+1)/(K+1) < N`, a shortfall of `(N−K)/(K+1)` — because a finite sample almost never contains the top of the range. The counterintuitive fix ADDS BACK the expected gap: the minimum-variance UNBIASED estimator EXTRAPOLATES ABOVE the largest observed serial, `N̂ = m(1 + 1/K) − 1 = m + (m−K)/K` = "sample maximum + the average gap between the ordered samples", which is exactly unbiased (`E[N̂]=N`) with exact variance `Var(N̂)=(N−K)(N+1)/(K(K+2))`. Because `m ≥ K` always, `N̂ ≥ m` always — the honest best estimate is at or above the largest serial observed, never below. It is the MVUE because `m` is a complete sufficient statistic (Lehmann–Scheffé). A competing unbiased estimator, twice-the-sample-mean-minus-one `Ñ = 2x̄ − 1`, is also unbiased but strictly LESS efficient: `Var(Ñ) = (N+1)(N−K)/(3K)`, so `Var(Ñ)/Var(N̂) = (K+2)/3` — at K=5 the mean-doubling estimator is `7/3 ≈ 2.33×` noisier, so the max-based estimator (which discards all but the largest serial) is the MORE efficient one. ## 💡 Session idea
"The best estimate of a bound is not the most extreme value you have seen — it is that value plus the gap the sampling left beyond it" is a reusable audit lens for any extreme-value or population-size inference read off a finite sample. Whenever an operator estimates a maximum / total / population size from sampled sequential labels — how many users/requests/invoices/SKUs exist behind sampled monotonic IDs, the fleet size behind observed asset tags, the run-length of a queue from sampled ticket numbers, the number of concurrent sessions from sampled tokens — the sample maximum is biased LOW by the expected gap above it, and the honest estimator EXTRAPOLATES past the largest observation by exactly that gap: `N̂ = m(1 + 1/K) − 1`. The transferable corrections: (1) never anchor a population/max estimate at the largest value observed — add the average sample gap and report the se from `(N−K)(N+1)/(K(K+2))`; (2) prefer the max-based correction to any mean-based estimator when the labels are roughly uniform, because the sample maximum is a SUFFICIENT statistic — the information you think the mean adds is already captured, and mean-doubling only adds variance (~2.33× at K=5); (3) mind the modelling caveat that made the WWII original work — the estimator assumes uniform, gap-free serial numbering, so serial resets / blocked allocations break it. Candidate future unrelated/estimation heads with the same "the estimator must extrapolate past the sample" spine: the Good–Turing missing-mass estimate (the probability the NEXT draw is a never-before-seen type is the singleton fraction, not zero), the capture–recapture / Lincoln–Petersen population estimator (N̂ = n₁n₂/m from tag-overlap), and the Laplace rule-of-succession correction (a run of successes does not put the failure probability at zero) — each a closed-form-anchored, single-control, "the naive point estimate is biased toward the observed extreme" claim.
