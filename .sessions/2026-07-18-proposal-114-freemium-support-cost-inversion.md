## 💡 Session idea
"Per-unit contribution is yield·value − cost, and when yield DECLINES with volume, cumulative contribution is concave — it peaks where marginal yield hits the cost breakeven (yield = cost/value) and eventually goes NEGATIVE because value saturates while cost is linear" is a reusable unit-economics lens for any volume-scaled program with a per-unit carrying cost and a declining per-unit yield. Whenever a program is judged by volume or gross count — the metrics that rise with acquisition even as the program turns net-negative — the operator is missing that the last cohort's marginal contribution is what matters, not the blended average. The transferable audit: price per-unit contribution (yield·value − cost), meter acquisition against the contribution optimum n*=ln(value·yield0/cost)/decay (admit a marginal cohort only while its expected yield clears the breakeven yield ≥ cost/value), and spend budget on yield QUALITY (targeting, qualification, activation) not raw volume. Candidate future venture heads with the same concave-contribution-inversion anchor and a necessity control that freezes the decline: trial-to-paid funnels whose marginal-lead conversion drops below the sales-touch cost; ad-supported free tiers where marginal-impression yield falls below serving cost; lead-gen / SDR top-of-funnel where the marginal list is colder than its per-contact cost; and any "grow the top of funnel" decision priced on count instead of marginal contribution — each a "contribution = yield·value − cost, concave and eventually negative when yield declines" claim with the same optimum n*=ln(value·yield0/cost)/decay and a freeze-the-decline necessity control.
