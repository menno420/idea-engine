Draft and land round-33 GAME-slot PROPOSAL 143 — rank-decay churn cliff. Folk belief: "rank decay keeps a ladder healthy — if players lose rank when they stop playing, decay pressures them to keep playing, so more decay = more activity." The SIGN of decay's effect on activity depends on λ: with no loss aversion (λ=1) decay helps (folk belief holds), with realistic loss aversion (λ=2.25) decay hurts. ## 💡 Session idea

The literal formal model handed down for P143 (fixed per-player enjoyment + idle-freeze under no decay) provably produces the FOLK result — decay HELPS activity in BOTH loss-aversion worlds — because a d=0 ladder freezes every player who ever declines a match, so decay reads as a pure anti-freeze stabilizer; the counterintuitive backfire only emerges once the baseline is a LIVING population (per-match enjoyment redrawn each period) so that decay's loss-averse churn can outrun its recovery channel. Candidate follow-up: a "baseline-degeneracy audit" discipline for every agent-based game/econ proposal in the fleet — before pinning gates, check that the NULL/control world is a non-degenerate steady state (does the no-intervention arm collapse to an absorbing state that trivially flatters the intervention?), because a frozen or absorbing baseline silently inverts the sign of the headline and turns a real behavioural claim into an artifact of the control's degeneracy.
