## 💡 Session idea
The proof pins a SINGLE homogeneous series chain — N=20 IDENTICAL Exp(λ=1.0) components, so every element contributes an equal 1/N share of the summed hazard λ_sys=∑λ_i=Nλ and the collapse is the clean symmetric T/N. But real reliability budgets are almost never homogeneous: a request path has one slow flaky hop among fast ones, a supply chain has a single-sourced part among commodity ones, an approval chain has one bottleneck signer. The load-bearing generalization the current gate does NOT exercise is HAZARD CONCENTRATION: for a HETEROGENEOUS series system with rates {λ_i}, the system is still exponential with rate ∑λ_i so MTBF = 1/∑λ_i, and the FRACTION of system failures attributable to component i is exactly λ_i/∑λ_i (the competing-exponentials / "which alarm fired first" identity) — so a single dominant element with λ_max ≫ the rest sets both the MTBF floor AND is the near-certain first-failure cause, which is precisely where a redundancy dollar buys the most. A genuinely different next UNRELATED slice pins the heterogeneous-chain reliability-budget mechanism: hold the min-of-exponentials frame but draw a SKEWED rate vector (e.g. one λ_max plus N−1 small λ) and pre-register (G-a) a Pareto/concentration gate — the measured share of system failures caused by the single dominant component matches the closed-form λ_max/∑λ_i within 3σ (so an operator can read "one part causes X% of all system failures" straight off the rates); (G-b) a budget-allocation gate — halving λ_max lifts system MTBF by a measured factor matching 1/(∑λ_i − λ_max/2)·(∑λ_i) within 3σ while halving a small λ_i barely moves it (≥3σ smaller lift), proving reliability spend belongs on the dominant hazard, not spread evenly; and (G-c) a redundancy-placement control — adding one parallel spare to the dominant component vs to a random small component, gated by a ≥3σ larger MTBF gain for the dominant placement — converting this slice's symmetric "seriesing divides MTBF by N" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "in a real chain one hazard dominates ∑λ_i, causes λ_max/∑λ_i of all failures, and is where every redundancy/shortening dollar should go" — a seed-pinnable heterogeneous-hazard-concentration mechanism distinct from this slice's homogeneous collapse and from the outbox's symmetric series/parallel contrast.
