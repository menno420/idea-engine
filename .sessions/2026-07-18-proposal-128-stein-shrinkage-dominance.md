Folk belief: you cannot beat it, and borrowing strength across UNRELATED coordinates (a batting average, the price of tea, a proton mass) is meaningless. Stein (1956) / James-Stein (1961): for p≥3 the MLE is INADMISSIBLE — θ̂_JS = target + (1 − (p−2)/S)·(X − target), S=||X−target||², shrinking every coordinate toward a common fixed point, has strictly SMALLER total risk E[Σ(θ̂_i−θ_i)²] than the MLE for EVERY θ. ## 💡 Session idea
"When you must estimate several unrelated quantities at once and you care about TOTAL accuracy, the separately-optimal answer is jointly beatable" is a reusable pooling-beats-isolation audit lens for any multi-target estimation surface — the pure statistical dual of the diffusion-of-responsibility lens from P127, but inverted: there a bigger substitute set HURT the collective; here a bigger set of simultaneous estimates HELPS, because shrinkage borrows strength across them. The transferable corrections: (1) count how many quantities you are estimating at once from noisy single reads — at ≥3 the sample-mean/MLE is inadmissible and a shrinkage estimator (James-Stein toward a common target, or empirical-Bayes toward the grand mean) strictly lowers total squared error, the gain scaling like (p−2)²·E[1/||X||²] with the number pooled; (2) the win does NOT require the quantities to be related — the improvement is a geometric fact about high-dimensional noise, so pooling a batting average with a candy-bar weight is legitimate when total MSE is the objective; (3) the target of shrinkage is a modeling choice (origin, grand mean, a regression fit) that trades a little per-coordinate bias for a large aggregate variance cut — pick it where the truths cluster. Candidate future unrelated/cross-cutting heads with the same "pool-to-dominate" spine: the positive-part James-Stein estimator (clamping the shrinkage factor at 0 for a further uniform improvement), the Efron-Morris shrink-toward-the-grand-mean variant (unknown common center), and the ridge/empirical-Bayes bridge (shrinkage as the MSE-optimal bias-variance trade under a Gaussian prior).
