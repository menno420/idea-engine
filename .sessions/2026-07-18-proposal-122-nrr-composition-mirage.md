On a concentrated book (a few large accounts owning most of the revenue) where whales expand and a long tail of small accounts churns, the whales' dollars dominate the numerator and blended NRR reads ABOVE 100% ("negative net churn", best-in-class) even though the MAJORITY OF LOGOS were lost — Simpson's paradox in a weighted mean. ## 💡 Session idea
"A retention number is a WEIGHTED mean — always ask what it is weighted by, and read a gap between two weightings as the concentration risk" is a reusable audit lens for any headline aggregate over a heterogeneous, concentrated population. Whenever an operator quotes a single blended metric over accounts/users/segments of wildly different size — net revenue retention, gross dollar retention, average order value, blended CAC/LTV, a portfolio yield, a marketplace take-rate — the weighting silently decides the answer, and a dollar-weighted (or size-weighted) aggregate can point the opposite way from the count-weighted one whenever the weights correlate with the quantity (Simpson's paradox in a weighted mean). The transferable corrections: (1) never report a size-weighted aggregate alone — pair it with the equal-weighted (per-entity) and the count-weighted view, and read the GAP between them as a concentration measure, not noise; (2) a size-weighted metric above its "healthy" line on a concentrated book is fragility, not strength — it lives or dies on a handful of large entities, so stress it against a whale-departure; (3) segment before you celebrate — the small-entity tail is invisible in the blended number exactly when it is bleeding. Candidate future venture/metric heads with the same "the aggregate hides the composition" spine: the AOV-mix Simpson trap (blended average order value rising while every channel's AOV falls, from mix shift toward a high-AOV channel); the blended-CAC payback illusion (a healthy blended CAC masking a channel whose marginal CAC has crossed LTV); and the cohort-blended churn-rate flattening (a stable pooled churn rate hiding a worsening new-cohort hazard, the flip side of P106's survivorship) — each a closed-form-or-simulable, single-mechanism, "the headline aggregate misreads the population" claim.
