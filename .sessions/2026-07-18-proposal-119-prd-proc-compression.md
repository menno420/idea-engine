## What happened
- Idea: PRD (Warcraft III / Dota 2) replaces a flat proc chance p with an escalating one — the chance starts at C and rises by C on every consecutive failure, P(proc on the n-th attempt since the last proc)=min(1,C·n), resetting to C on a proc — which bounds bad-luck streaks (chance hits 1 by attempt ⌈1/C⌉) and smooths good-luck streaks. The counterintuitive trap: the increment constant C is NOT the proc rate. The number of attempts between procs N has E[N]=Σ n·P(N=n) with P(N=n)=[∏_{k<n}(1−min(1,C·k))]·min(1,C·n), and the long-run EFFECTIVE proc rate is 1/E[N]. For C=0.25 the walk terminates at n=4 (C·4=1.0): P(N=1)=0.25, P(N=2)=0.375, P(N=3)=0.28125, P(N=4)=0.09375, E[N]=2.21875, effective rate=1/2.21875=0.450704 — nearly DOUBLE 0.25. A designer who naively sets C=nominal ships a proc that fires almost twice as often as intended; the fix is to SOLVE C for the target rate (C≈0.0847 for 0.25). ## 💡 Session idea
"The parameter that shapes an escalating-chance RNG is not its long-run rate" is a reusable design lens for any anti-streak / bad-luck-protection mechanic (PRD crit/bash/evasion, gacha soft-pity ramps, momentum-based drop tables): whenever a per-attempt chance is made to CLIMB with consecutive failures, the long-run realized frequency is 1/E[N] of the resulting inter-success walk, which sits ABOVE the increment constant — so a designer who reads the tuning knob as "the rate" ships a mechanic that fires far more often than intended. The transferable audit: for any state-dependent proc, SOLVE the increment/curve for the target long-run rate (analytically via E[N] or by simulation), never set the knob equal to the nominal; and treat the tightened bad-luck-streak bound (⌈1/C⌉−1 misses for PRD) as the FEATURE you are actually buying, priced separately from the rate. Candidate future game heads with the same "state-dependent chance → 1/E[N] effective rate" anchor: a soft-pity gacha ramp whose effective pull-rate exceeds its base; a momentum drop table whose escalating bonus inflates realized drops; a comeback-mechanic whose per-loss buff overshoots its intended win-rate lift — each a "the curve is not the rate" claim with an E[N] closed form and a same-target solved-parameter control.
