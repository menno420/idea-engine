## Objective

Draft and land round-32 VENTURE-slot PROPOSAL 138 — the usage-based billing variance shock: under consumption pricing a firm's monthly revenue R = Σ Uᵢ is a sum over N independent accounts, so the folk law-of-large-numbers model predicts the coefficient of variation falls like CV_account/√N. That is FALSE when account SIZES are heterogeneous: independent-but-unequal accounts add in variance weighted by size², so CV(R) = CV_account·√HHI with HHI = Σwᵢ² the Herfindahl concentration index and N_eff = 1/HHI the effective account count, NOT N. With Zipf sizes (mᵢ = 1/i, i=1..400) a 400-account book has N_eff ≈ 26 — revenue is ~4× more volatile than the LLN predicts, a shock the account count hides because size concentration (Cauchy–Schwarz forces HHI ≥ 1/N), not customer count, sets the variance floor. ## 💡 Session idea
