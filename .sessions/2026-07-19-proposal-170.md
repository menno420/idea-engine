## GROUNDING (verified at HEAD)
Winner's curse (Capen, Clapp & Campbell 1971; common-value auction theory): when N bidders form unbiased but noisy estimates of a common value and the highest bid wins, the winner's estimate is the maximum order statistic and thus biased high — the winner systematically overpays. Applied to venture: a hot round with many competing term sheets is a common-value auction on the company's intrinsic value. Reference resolved live this session.
