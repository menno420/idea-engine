## 💡 Session idea
The proof pins a SINGLE static cap C against a stationary Poisson login process and shows usable fraction 1−e^{−C/μ}. But operators almost never run a static cap — they run cap BOOSTS (limited-time "double energy cap" events) precisely to re-engage lapsed/absent players, exactly the μ-large cohort this proof shows the base cap taxes hardest. A genuinely different next GAME slice pins the boost as a swept axis: over the same Poisson-gap draws, sweep a temporary cap multiplier k ∈ {1, 2, 4, ∞} applied for a fraction f of the horizon, and pre-register two gates: (1) the incremental usable fraction ΔW(k) recovered by the boost is strictly LARGER for the casual cohort (μ=480) than the frequent cohort (μ=60) at ≥3σ — i.e. the boost is PROGRESSIVE where the base cap is regressive, quantifying who a cap-boost event actually reaches; and (2) the boost's capture still saturates — ΔW(k=∞) − ΔW(k=2) is bounded below the G3 noise floor, showing even an unbounded temporary cap cannot recover the e^{−C/μ} forfeited during the un-boosted fraction 1−f, so the fix is banking overflow into a reserve, not episodic cap inflation. That converts the flat "raise the cap saturates fast" into the actionable "cap-boost events are a progressive but bounded palliative — the durable fix is an uncapped overflow reserve or cadence-scaled cap," a seed-pinnable, ≥3σ-gateable mechanism (cap-boost incidence curve) distinct from this slice's static-cap forfeiture result.
