# VERDICT 144 mirror — the single-elimination favorite-collapse / competitive-format design · order statistics: the folk model of a knockout bracket is that a bigger/longer tournament is a truer test that rewards the best player — "add more rounds and the cream rises." It is INVERTED. In a single-elimination bracket the strongest entrant must win EVERY round, so its title probability is the PRODUCT of its per-match win probabilities — a quantity that shrinks geometrically as the bracket grows. Model each entrant by a Bradley-Terry strength θ; a match between strengths a and b is won by a with probability a/(a+b). Put one favorite at strength f>1 and a field of N−1 identical "normal" entrants at strength 1; because the normals are indistinguishable the favorite draws a normal EVERY round under ANY seeding, so its per-match win probability is the constant p=f/(f+1) and its exact title probability is the closed form P_title(R)=p^R over R=log2(N) rounds — strictly decreasing in R, each added round an independent coin-weighted haircut that can only LOWER the favorite's odds. With f=3 (a 3× favorite, p=0.75): R=4 (16 players)→31.6%, R=6 (64-player esports bracket)→17.8%, R=8 (256 players)→10.0% — the best player is dethroned in the large majority of runs and it gets WORSE the "truer" you make the test; seeding alone does NOT fix it (under indistinguishable normals the favorite draws a normal every round regardless of layout).
