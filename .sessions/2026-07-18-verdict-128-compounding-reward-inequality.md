## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the inequality on a STATIONARY, HOMOGENEOUS multiplicative process — every player draws the SAME iid Normal(MU_G, SIGMA_G) daily log-return, so terminal wealth is exactly log-normal with a single scale-free parameter σ_T=SIGMA_G·√DAYS and the concentration is a clean closed form (Gini=2Φ(σ_T/√2)−1), unbounded in the horizon — and the correction it proves ("pay on a fixed base / cap the horizon / rebase") is exact precisely because the process has no floor, no reinjection, and no cross-player coupling. But real game economies almost never let wealth compound freely to infinity: they SINK it (consumption, decay, upkeep/taxes that scale with holdings) and they FLOOR it (a UBI-style daily grant, a bankruptcy reset, a soft-cap diminishing-returns curve on the multiplier). A multiplicative process with a proportional sink and an additive reinjection is no longer log-normal-to-infinity — it is a Kesten process, whose stationary wealth distribution is POWER-LAW (Pareto) with a tail exponent set by the ratio of the multiplicative variance to the sink/reinjection strength, so inequality STABILIZES at a finite Gini instead of diverging. The load-bearing generalization the current gate does NOT exercise is that the design lever is not binary (compound vs additive) but CONTINUOUS: a small proportional wealth sink (a "% of holdings" upkeep/decay, the mirror image of a "% of holdings" bonus) plus a flat floor grant converts the divergent log-normal into a convergent power law and TUNES the steady-state Gini to a target. A genuinely different next GAME slice pins the Kesten-economy mechanism: hold the multiplicative daily update W←W·exp(g) but add a proportional sink (W←(1−τ)·W) and a flat floor grant (W←W+b), draw to the stationary distribution, and pre-register (G-a) a tail-law gate — the stationary wealth tail is Pareto with exponent α matching the closed-form Kesten root (E[((1−τ)e^g)^α]=1) within 3σ, NOT log-normal (a divergent-vs-convergent discriminator); (G-b) a Gini-stabilization gate — under the sink+floor the Gini converges to a finite steady state independent of the horizon (mean Gini at DAYS vs 2·DAYS within 3σ), whereas the pure-compound control's Gini keeps rising with the horizon by ≥3σ (isolating the sink as what bounds inequality); and (G-c) a lever-monotonicity gate — the steady-state Gini is monotone decreasing in the sink rate τ by ≥3σ across a τ-grid (so an operator can dial a target Gini with the upkeep rate) — converting this slice's binary "never uncapped compounding" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "add a proportional sink + floor to turn a divergent log-normal economy into a convergent power-law one whose Gini you tune with the sink rate" — a seed-pinnable Kesten-process economics mechanism distinct from this slice's floorless homogeneous log-normal and from the outbox's compound-vs-additive single-lever frame.
