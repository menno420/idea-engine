## 💡 Session idea
The proof pins a SINGLE convergent rating system (symmetric Elo, single K=16, one burn-in) and gates the win-rate mirage at the CONVERGED regime — win rate measured only after 400 burn-in rounds, so ratings sit at their σ(0)=0.5 fixed point. But the proposal's own model-basis note flags a load-bearing TRANSIENT: "a season too short would leave ratings far from equilibrium and INFLATE corr(skill,winrate) — the shorter the convergence, the less compression." That transient is not just a caveat to burn away — it is a MEASURABLE, operationally decisive regime, because a real ladder is never fully converged (new players, smurfs, seasonal resets, rating decay all inject fresh disequilibrium continuously). A genuinely different next GAME slice pins the CONVERGENCE-TRANSIENT of the mirage: hold the same SBMM Elo season but SWEEP the burn-in / games-played axis and gate win-rate informativeness as a function of games played, pre-registering (G-a) a monotone-decay gate — corr(skill, win rate) and the decile gap DECREASE monotonically with games-played toward the converged floor by ≥3σ per sweep step (win rate is MOST informative early, and matchmaking progressively erases it as the rating converges — the exact opposite of "more games = more reliable win rate"); (G-b) a half-life gate — the number of games at which the win-rate decile gap has decayed halfway from its random-matching level to its converged floor is finite and reproduced within 3σ against a closed-form / fitted exponential-approach constant tied to K and SKILL_SD (so an operator can compute "win rate is trustworthy for the first N games, then becomes a mirage"); and (G-c) a smurf/reset control — inject a batch of mis-rated players (true skill ≫ current rating) and show their TRANSIENT win rate spikes far above 0.5 and decays to 0.5 as the rating climbs, gateable by a ≥3σ transient-surplus a converged player never shows — proving a sustained win-rate surplus is a rating-disequilibrium signal (still climbing), exactly as the proposal asserts but does not gate. That converts this slice's converged-regime "win rate is a mirage, grade by rating" into the actionable, ≥3σ-gateable "win rate is informative ONLY in the transient, with a computable half-life; a win-rate surplus is a not-yet-converged-rating signal, so grade by rating velocity early and by rating level once converged" — a seed-pinnable convergence-transient mechanism distinct from this slice's converged-regime compression and from the outbox's static two-regime contrast.
