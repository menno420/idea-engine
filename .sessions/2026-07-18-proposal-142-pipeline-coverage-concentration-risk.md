## Objective

Draft and land round-33 VENTURE-slot PROPOSAL 142 — pipeline coverage concentration risk. The sales "pipeline coverage ratio" (expected pipeline value ÷ quota) is NOT a sufficient statistic for quota attainment. For bookings B = Σ Xᵢvᵢ with iid Bernoulli(p) win indicators Xᵢ over deals of value vᵢ (total V), E[B] = p·V and Var(B) = p(1−p)·Σvᵢ² = p(1−p)·V²·HHI, where HHI = Σwᵢ² is the Herfindahl concentration of deal-value shares wᵢ = vᵢ/V. Coverage E[B]/QUOTA fixes ONLY the first moment (p and V), so two pipelines with the same p and same V have identical coverage and identical expected bookings — yet at fixed p and V the variance RATIO equals the HHI RATIO exactly (p(1−p) and V² cancel), so a book concentrated in a few whales (high HHI) misses quota far more often than a granular book (HHI → 1/N floor) at the SAME coverage.
