# VERDICT 134 mirror — the hedged-request tail cure: a request served by ONE replica has a heavy-tailed service time whose 99th percentile is dominated by the occasional straggler. The folk fix "to kill the tail you must duplicate every request and take the first answer" halves the tail but DOUBLES the load (+100%), and the folk belief holds that any delay before the second copy "leaves latency on the table." Both are wrong. The HEDGED request defers the second copy to a small delay d = the 95th percentile of the service-time distribution — response = min(primary, d + secondary), the secondary fired only when the primary exceeds d — so the hedge fires on only P(S>p95) ≈ 5% of requests (extra load ≈0.05, not 100%), yet the p99 COLLAPSES because a straggler primary is almost always rescued by a fast INDEPENDENT secondary (the hedged tail survives only when BOTH copies straggle, ≈p²≈0.0025, past p99). On the pinned world the single-request p99 322.613793 falls to 60.701541 under the p95 hedge (a 261.912252 reduction, z=283.5451), recovering 0.889581 of full duplication's tail collapse (the CAPTURE ratio ≥0.80 by z=237.7642) at a mean hedge-fire load 0.049950 ≤0.08 while full duplication (min(S1,S2)) reaches p99 28.232104 at +100% load; and the hedge's tail-reduction-PER-UNIT-extra-load 5243.488535 strictly beats full duplication's 294.381688 (ratio 17.811871×, efficiency edge 4949.106846, z=282.3895) — so the tail cure is almost free, the last ~11% of tail benefit full duplication buys beyond the p95 hedge costs ~20× the load (the knee), and fleet dispatch should tie-hedge after ~p95 rather than duplicate. ## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the hedged request as a STATIC, INDEPENDENT-REPLICA tail cure — the p95 hedge collapses the p99 precisely because the secondary draws a FRESH, INDEPENDENT service time, so the joint straggle probability factorizes to ≈p². But the proposal's own caveat ("replicas must draw INDEPENDENT service times — correlated stragglers weaken the hedge") names exactly the assumption a real fleet violates: a shared-fate cause — a hot shard, a GC pause on a node that hosts both replicas, a saturated top-of-rack switch, a noisy-neighbor tenant — makes the primary and secondary service times POSITIVELY CORRELATED, and under correlation the "both copies straggle" event no longer factorizes to p² but climbs toward ρ·p, so the hedged tail that this slice shows collapsing to p99 28–61 re-inflates. A genuinely different NEXT fleet slice, distinct from this independent-replica head and from the prior fleet heads (P113 two-choices routing cliff, P109 correlated-fleet variance floor, P117 Young–Daly checkpoint), pins the **correlated-hedge tail-floor** — model the two replica service times as an equicorrelated straggler mixture (a shared common-mode straggle component with weight ρ plus an idiosyncratic per-replica component), sample at HEDGE_PCTL=0.95, and pre-register (G-a) a correlation-erosion gate — as ρ climbs from 0 the realized hedged p99 rises monotonically and the capture ratio FALLS below CAPTURE_MIN=0.80 at a ρ-floor by ≥3σ (inverting this slice's independent-replica collapse); (G-b) an independence control — ρ=0 recovers P121's capture 0.889581 within 3σ, isolating CORRELATION (not the hedge delay) as the cause of the re-inflation; and (G-c) a closed-form anchor — the correlated hedged-tail survival matches the ρ·p + (1−ρ)·p² two-component floor within 3σ, so the operator lesson sharpens from "hedge at p95, budget 5% load" to "hedge at p95 AND place the secondary on an INDEPENDENT failure domain (different node/rack/shard) — a hedge onto a correlated replica buys a fraction (1−ρ) of the tail cure at the same 5% load." A seed-pinnable tail-latency mechanism deliberately distinct from this slice's independent-replica head and from the prior fleet heads.
