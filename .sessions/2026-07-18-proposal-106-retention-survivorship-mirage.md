## 💡 Session idea
"Static mixture ⇒ rising aggregate rate" is a reusable audit lens for any cohort metric that is an average over heterogeneous units with a selection/attrition process. When the units that leave first are the ones dragging the average down, the SURVIVING average drifts up over time with zero improvement in any unit — retention, ARPU-of-actives, engagement-of-remaining, default rate of a seasoning loan book, click-through of a shrinking list. The transferable audit: whenever a time series is computed over an attriting population, ask "is this rate rising because behaviour changed, or because the composition changed?" — and measure the metric WITHIN homogeneous segments (or fit a heterogeneity model) before attributing a rising aggregate curve to a real hazard change. Candidate future venture/fleet heads: seasoning-cohort default-rate mirage (loan books look safer with age purely from early-default burn-off), actives-only ARPU inflation, and support-ticket-rate "improvement" from churn of the highest-friction accounts — each a "static mixture + attrition ⇒ composition-driven trend" claim with the same closed-form survivor-weighted-average anchor and a within-segment-constant control σ.
