## What this proposal does

Adds a fleet PROPOSAL establishing the **triangular-number fixed-point law** of **Bulgarian solitaire**, a deterministic dynamical system on integer partitions of `n`. The move: remove one card from **every** pile, then form **ONE** new pile whose size = the number of piles you had (`step(p) = sort_desc([x−1 for x in p if x−1>0] + [len(p)])`). (1) If `n = T_k = k(k+1)/2` is **triangular**, EVERY partition of `T_k` converges to the **unique period-1 fixed point**, the staircase `δ_k = (k, k−1, …, 1)`; (2) the **maximum** steps-to-fixed-point over ALL partitions of `T_k` is **EXACTLY `k²−k`** (Wikipedia gives "`k²−k` or fewer" — the exact tightness is derived here by enumeration); (3) if `n` is **not** triangular, no fixed point exists and every orbit lands in a **period>1 limit cycle**. Headline `k=6, n=21`: all 792 partitions reach `δ_6=(6,5,4,3,2,1)`, `M(6)=30=6²−6`, uniform-random mean `μ≈20.17` steps. Smallest non-triangular witness `n=2`: cycle `(1,1)↔(2)`. **Distinctness:** a self-map on partitions and its orbit structure — no opponent, no nim-sum, no `mex`, no game tree; the conserved invariant is the card total `n`. Orthogonal to every shipped game head — Sprague-Grundy nim-sum (P219), Wythoff (P239), Green Hackenbush (P247), Fibonacci-Nim (P243), Penney, Banzhaf.
