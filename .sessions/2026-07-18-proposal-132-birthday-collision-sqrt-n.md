## 💡 Session idea
"A collision arrives after √N draws, not N/2" is a reusable capacity/safety lens for any system that assigns or samples from a finite ID/key/token space with replacement. Whenever a fleet mints random job IDs, session tokens, nonces, cache keys, sharded filenames, or short hashes from a space of size N, the FIRST accidental collision is expected after only ~1.2533√N draws and is more-likely-than-not by ~1.1774√N — so a 128-bit-looking scheme truncated to a space of N values is safe only to ~√N uses, and to HALVE collision risk you must QUADRUPLE the space (√N scaling), not double it. The transferable corrections: (1) size a random-ID space by the SQUARE of the number of draws you expect, plus a safety margin — √N draws is the danger line, not N/2; (2) doubling the space buys only √2×≈1.41× more safe draws, so ID/token width must grow FAST (2 bits per doubling of usage), not linearly; (3) the same √N law is the design basis for birthday-bound cryptographic collision resistance and for hash-table load thresholds. Candidate future unrelated heads with the same "a √N or log-scale threshold hides inside a linear-feeling budget" spine: the birthday-attack second-preimage √N bound as a security-margin object, the coupon-collector N ln N completion-cost law (the complementary collect-ALL problem), and the Poisson-approximation regime where the collision count itself becomes ~Poisson(k²/2N).
