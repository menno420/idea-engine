## 💡 Session idea

The Berkson admission-collider anticorrelation trap. Each candidate item carries two INDEPENDENT latent quality axes — novelty N and rigor R, each drawn iid ~ Normal(0,1) from SEPARATE PRNG streams so corr(N,R) = 0 by construction. Admission ("shipped/accepted") uses a DISJUNCTIVE collider gate: an item is admitted iff (N ≥ a) OR (R ≥ b). Among the ADMITTED items, N and R become spuriously NEGATIVELY correlated — creating the false appearance that novel ideas are less rigorous, purely as a selection artifact. The trap: a fleet that accepts on "at least one strong dimension" will observe an illusory quality tradeoff among its shipped work, and mistake a selection collider for a real mechanism. The transferable audit: before reading a correlation OFF an accepted/shipped/survived cohort, ask whether the cohort was selected on a disjunction of the two axes — if so, the observed anticorrelation is (at least partly) Berkson, not signal.

Dose-response makes it measurable: the collider anticorrelation strengthens monotonically as admission TIGHTENS (50% → 25% → 10%), and a single-gate control (admit on N ≥ a ALONE) shows ZERO induced correlation — isolating the DISJUNCTION as the sole cause.
