## 💡 Session idea
This slice proves the Jensen-convexity blending bias for LTV = m/c (convex in the churn rate c, so a blended churn UNDERSTATES the true portfolio LTV). The same convexity trap should distort a second, operationally distinct headline metric that operators ALSO compute off a blended input: the **CAC-payback period T = CAC/m** is convex in the per-customer contribution margin m (T = CAC·(1/m), and 1/m is convex), so collapsing a heterogeneous book to one blended margin m̄ and reading payback as CAC/m̄ UNDERSTATES the true average payback E[CAC/m] ≥ CAC/m̄ — i.e. the blended number makes the book look like it pays back FASTER than the cohort-average customer actually does, the OPPOSITE sign of the LTV bias and therefore the more dangerous of the two (LTV blending leaves profit on the table; payback blending greenlights cash-flow-fragile acquisition). Pre-registrable, seed-pinned, stdlib-only in the same shape as this verifier: (G-a) understatement bias — with margins m~Uniform on a wide band (fixed mean m̄), the MC mean of CAC/m exceeds the blended CAC/m̄ by ≥3σ; (G-b) convex closed-form match — the MC mean payback lands within 3σ of CAC·E[1/m]=CAC·ln(b/a)/(b−a) over a uniform band, not the blended CAC/m̄; (G-c) dispersion-isolation control — a same-mean narrow margin band collapses the payback gap toward 0 (gap ≈ CAC·Var(m)/m̄³), proving margin DISPERSION not level drives it. The transferable operator correction sharpens from "a heterogeneous book is worth MORE than its blended churn says" (this slice, LTV) to "and it pays back SLOWER than its blended margin says — never size an LTV/CAC bar OR a payback/cash-runway plan off a pooled input, because the same convexity that flatters LTV punishes payback; compute both per segment and average the segment values." (The blended discount-rate/NPV variant is deliberately NOT taken — it overlaps the P130 annual-prepay-trap surface, which already carries per-customer heterogeneous cost-of-capital r_i and an NPV/IRR treatment; the payback direction is the cleaner deduped pick.)
