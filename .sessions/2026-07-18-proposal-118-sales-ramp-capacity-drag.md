## What happened
- Idea: a new sales rep does not book at full quota on day one — productivity ramps from zero to full quota Q over a ramp period R (onboarding, territory build, pipeline maturation), modelled linearly as q(a)=Q·min(a/R,1) for a rep of tenure a. In a force growing at monthly rate g whose reps leave at attrition rate d, the steady-state tenure of the CURRENTLY-ACTIVE force is exponential with rate β=g+d (Lotka stable-population age structure: cohort hired a months ago numbered ~e^{g(t−a)}, survivors to now ~e^{−d·a}, so active tenure density is (g+d)·e^{−(g+d)a}). Averaging the linear ramp over that distribution gives the realized fraction of nominal quota the force actually books, φ(β)=E[min(a/R,1)]=(1−e^{−βR})/(βR) — the classic (1−e^{−x})/x form, always <1 and DECREASING in β. Two consequences: (1) nominal capacity (headcount·quota) overstates realized bookings by 1/φ and the overstatement WIDENS the faster you grow; (2) growth and attrition are INTERCHANGEABLE — φ depends only on β=g+d, so a +Δ over-hiring problem and a +Δ retention problem inflict the identical capacity drag and are indistinguishable from the aggregate productivity number. VENTURE-domain (sales-capacity planning / GTM unit economics), distinct from the referral amplifier (P098), the cash-flow-timing trough (P102), the retention-survivorship mirage (P106), the single-period discount margin (P110) and the freemium concave-contribution inversion (P114).

## 💡 Session idea
"Realized capacity is nominal capacity times a ramp-averaged utilization φ=(1−e^{−βR})/(βR), where β is the total turnover rate of the pool (inflow growth + outflow attrition) and R is the time to full productivity" is a reusable capacity-planning lens for ANY pool whose members ramp to full output over a warm-up period: sales reps (this slice), support agents, delivery/eng squads, content creators, even machines with a burn-in/tuning period. Two transferable morals: (1) plan on realized φ·nominal, not nominal — and note the overstatement 1/φ widens with growth, so aggressive scale-up buys less capacity than the headcount implies; (2) since φ depends only on β=g+d, reducing attrition is EXACTLY equivalent to slowing over-hiring — the two levers are interchangeable and a falling per-member productivity number cannot, on its own, tell a hiring problem from a retention problem. Candidate future heads with the same ramp-averaged-utilization anchor and a β-interchangeability control: support-agent ramp under queue growth + agent churn; onboarding-cohort activation where a growing user base dilutes the "% activated" snapshot; capacity of any warm-up pool (nodes with cache-warm, models with fine-tune) under scaling + replacement — each a "realized = nominal·(1−e^{−βR})/(βR), β=inflow+outflow" claim with a matched-outflow interchangeability control.
