## What this proposal does
Adds a fleet PROPOSAL establishing the exact closed form for Buffon's needle in the short-needle regime (ℓ ≤ d): a needle of length ℓ dropped uniformly at random on a plane ruled with parallel lines spaced d apart crosses a line with probability EXACTLY P = 2ℓ/(πd). Ships a stdlib-only firsthand verifier that implements the DROP as the geometric Bernoulli event u ≤ (ℓ/2)·sinθ (center offset u ~ Uniform[0, d/2], angle θ ~ Uniform[0, π/2]) and does NOT assume the closed form — the 2/π angle average is exactly what the Monte-Carlo measures, and the exact rational crossing kernel is pinned with fractions.Fraction. Fills a confirmed gap: Buffon's needle is grep-0 across ideas/ (it was literally named as one of the two "unoccupied classics" — with Monty Hall — in the hundred-prisoners session-idea list) and is mechanistically distinct from the shipped counterintuitive-probability heads (birthday/coupon collisions, inspection/arcsine renewal paths, Berkson/Simpson conditioning, giant-component thresholds): this is continuous integral-geometry, the appearance of π from a rotationally symmetric orientation law.

## Method
Exact rational arithmetic (fractions.Fraction) + an i.i.d. Monte-Carlo. The short-needle reduction: by symmetry the center offset to the nearest line is u ~ Uniform[0, d/2] and the acute angle to the lines is θ ~ Uniform[0, π/2]; the needle crosses iff u ≤ (ℓ/2)·sinθ, whose conditional probability given θ is min(1, (ℓ/d)·sinθ). Averaging over θ gives (ℓ/d)·E[sinθ] = (ℓ/d)·(2/π) = 2ℓ/(πd). G1 pins the exact rational kernel: over ℓ/d∈{1/4,1/2,3/4,1} and rational (Pythagorean) sines s∈{0,1/2,3/5,4/5,1}, the direct form min(1,(ℓ·s)/d) equals the offset-area form ((ℓ/2)·s)/(d/2) clamped to 1 exactly (identity_mismatches=0), and the exact expectation factorization E[cross]=(ℓ/d)·mean(s) matches two independent computations (expectation_mismatches=0) — an off-by-2 in the offset normalization or a sin-vs-|sin| bug fails this exactly. G2 draws N=2,000,000 i.i.d. drops at ℓ/d=1/2 (P=1/π) and runs a binomial-proportion z-test (|z|<3); the drops are i.i.d. Bernoulli so no thinning is needed. G3 verifies scale invariance (P depends only on ℓ/d) across k∈{2,5,0.3,100} and parametrization invariance (full circle [0,2π) with |sinθ| == [0,π/2]), max|z|<3. G4 rejects the naive "no angle factor" P=ℓ/d=0.5 at |z|≈514 and the subtler "E[sinθ]=½" P=0.25 at |z|≈223 on the SAME sample.
