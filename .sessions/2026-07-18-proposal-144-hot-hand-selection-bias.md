## Objective

Draft and land round-33 UNRELATED-slot PROPOSAL 144 — the hot-hand streak-selection bias (Miller & Sanjurjo, Econometrica 2018). Folk belief: "a fair, memoryless coin has no memory, so the fraction of heads on the flips that come right after a streak of heads must be exactly 0.5." ## 💡 Session idea

The verifier pins a SINGLE streak length (K=3) and a SINGLE sequence length (N=15) and gates only that the head fraction sits below 0.5 and matches the enumerated null. The load-bearing generalization the gates do NOT touch is the **magnitude law** the trap actually turns on: the bias is not a fixed constant — it scales UP with K and DOWN with N, and an ops audit needs the *size* of the correction, not just its sign. A genuinely different next unrelated slice pins that (N, K) → bias surface: hold the fair coin fixed, enumerate the exact expectation across a grid of (N, K), and pre-register (G-a) a monotone-in-K gate — at fixed N the bias magnitude strictly increases with the streak length K, ≥3σ; (G-b) a vanishing-in-N gate — at fixed K the bias magnitude strictly decreases as N grows and →0, converting "the null is below 0.5" into "the null is below 0.5 by *this computable amount*"; and (G-c) a correction-recovery gate — subtracting the enumerated null from a synthetic sequence carrying a KNOWN injected positive autocorrelation recovers the injected effect within ≥3σ, proving the debias is not just directional but *quantitatively* actionable. That turns this slice's "benchmark against the enumerated null, not 0.5" into a full correction curve a fleet streakiness-audit could apply to any finite window — the exact price of the law of small numbers, tabulated. A seed-free, fully-exact (2^N enumeration) mechanism distinct from this slice's single-point head and from the regression-to-the-mean card.
