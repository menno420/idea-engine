## What happened
- Idea: a majority vote is usually read as self-correcting — if each of N agents is better than a coin flip (p>1/2), the Condorcet Jury Theorem says the majority is correct with probability → 1 as N→∞, so a bigger quorum / more monitors / more ensemble members is more reliability. Reasoned to its fuller form (Q-0254): that promise holds ONLY under INDEPENDENCE. Real fleets share an error source — one stale upstream, one training prior, one correlated feature — so their mistakes arrive together and the majority cannot outvote an error the majority is making. Model the shared source with a one-factor latent-Gaussian probit: agent i's latent score `X_i=√ρ·C+√(1−ρ)·ε_i` (C, ε_i ~ N(0,1) iid), vote CORRECT iff `X_i>τ` with `τ=Φ⁻¹(1−p)` so the marginal competence `P(correct)=Φ(−τ)=p` holds for every agent regardless of ρ, and the pairwise latent error-correlation is ρ. Conditioning on the common factor C, votes are independent with per-agent correct-prob `q(C)=Φ((√ρ·C−τ)/√(1−ρ))`; by the LLN the fraction correct → q(C), so as N→∞ the majority is correct iff `C>τ/√ρ`, giving the closed-form CEILING `A_∞=Φ(Φ⁻¹(p)/√ρ)<1` for every ρ>0 (→1 only as ρ→0, the Condorcet limit). Correlation does not slow the climb, it CAPS it; the effective crowd saturates near N≈1/ρ.

## 💡 Session idea
"An aggregate over many agents is only as good as their INDEPENDENCE — always ask what error source the agents SHARE before crediting redundancy, because a common factor caps the aggregate no matter how many you add." Whenever a decision is a pooled function of many noisy units (a majority/quorum vote, an ensemble average, a k-of-N agreement, a monitor consensus), the folk model prices each added unit as an independent draw whose error cancels — but a shared error component `ρ` makes the units behave like an effective crowd of only ~`1/ρ`, and the pooled quantity converges to a correlation-set limit strictly short of the independent ideal (here the majority accuracy ceilings at `Φ(Φ⁻¹(p)/√ρ)<1`; the sibling in estimation, P109, is the MSE flooring at `ρσ²`). The transferable corrections: (1) never size a quorum/ensemble by count alone — measure the residual error-correlation `ρ` after conditioning on the input, and read the correlation-limited ceiling/floor as your real reliability; (2) past `N≈1/ρ` more units are near-worthless, so the lever is DECORRELATION (independent inputs, priors, data paths, upstreams), which lowers `ρ` and lifts the ceiling — adding correlated units cannot; (3) the same "shared-factor caps the aggregate" spine recurs across domains — majority-vote accuracy (this), averaging MSE (P109), and any wisdom-of-crowds surface — so the diagnostic question "what do these agents fail on TOGETHER?" generalizes far past voting. Candidate future heads on the same spine: the correlated-sensor false-alarm floor (a quorum of alarms sharing a common disturbance), the ensemble-model calibration ceiling under a shared training prior, and the replicated-storage durability wall under correlated failure domains.
