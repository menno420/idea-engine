# VERDICT 140 mirror — guild-contribution free-riding / the volunteer's dilemma: a guild/raid "someone-must-do-it" chore (soak a mechanic, farm the reagent, staff the event, hit a shared contribution threshold) is a binary public good provided iff AT LEAST ONE of N members pays a private cost c for a common benefit b (0<c<b). The folk belief "more members ⇒ someone will surely do it" INVERTS under the symmetric mixed-strategy Nash equilibrium: each member is indifferent between volunteering and free-riding, which pins p*(N)=1−(c/b)^{1/(N−1)}, so the probability NOBODY volunteers is P_fail(N)=(1−p*)^N=(c/b)^{N/(N−1)} — INCREASING in N toward c/b because the exponent N/(N−1) falls to 1. Bigger guild → HIGHER chance the collective task fails: the bystander effect / diffusion of responsibility as an equilibrium of self-interested waiting, not apathy. On the pinned world (b=1, c=0.5) P_fail runs 0.25 (N=2) → 0.46 (N=10) → 0.48 (N=20) toward the ceiling c/b=0.5, while the expected volunteer count N·p*(N) stays essentially FLAT near −ln(c/b)=0.693 — twenty members supply the same ~0.7 expected volunteers as a tuned pair, so recruiting is not insurance; the cure removes the substitutability (assign a named role, reward the volunteer a private surplus, or make the good individually rewarded). ## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the volunteer's dilemma on a SYMMETRIC roster — every member shares the identical cost/benefit (c=0.5, b=1) and therefore the identical equilibrium volunteer probability p*(N). The clean (c/b)^{N/(N−1)} non-provision law and the flat −ln(c/b) total effort are artifacts of that symmetry. A genuinely different NEXT game slice, distinct from this symmetric head and from the prior game heads (P091 gacha pity, P095 rubber-band controller, P099 shop-reroll-ruin, P103 streak-shield, P107 energy-cap overflow, P111 matchmaking mirage, P115 compounding-reward inequality, P119 PRD proc-rate, P123 snipe-clearing leak), pins the **ASYMMETRIC volunteer's dilemma / low-cost-player exploitation**: give members heterogeneous private costs c_i (a distribution over the roster — some find the chore cheap, others dear). The asymmetric equilibrium is known to concentrate provision on the LOWEST-cost member (Diekmann 1993, "Cooperation in an Asymmetric Volunteer's Dilemma Game"): as heterogeneity widens, the high-cost members free-ride toward zero volunteer probability and the single cheapest member carries the guild — an "exploitation of the great by the small" that (G-a) LOWERS the aggregate non-provision probability versus the symmetric-cost guild at the same mean cost (heterogeneity HELPS coverage — a pre-registrable ≥3σ gap), while (G-b) it concentrates the BURDEN: the cheapest member's volunteer probability and expected cost paid both rise with roster size and heterogeneity (a Gini-of-effort gate, ≥3σ), and (G-c) a symmetric-cost control at the same mean recovers THIS slice's exact (c/b)^{N/(N−1)} law within 3σ (isolating COST HETEROGENEITY, not the mean, as the cause). The operator lesson sharpens from "assign or reward the volunteer" to "a heterogeneous guild silently conscripts its lowest-cost member — coverage looks fine while one player burns out; measure the effort-Gini, not just the coverage rate, and rotate or compensate the load-bearing member before they quit." A seed-pinnable game mechanism deliberately distinct from this symmetric head and from the prior game closers.
