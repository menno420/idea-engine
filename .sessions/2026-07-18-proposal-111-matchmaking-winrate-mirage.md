## What happened
- Idea: under skill-based matchmaking players are paired by current Elo rating, and the symmetric Elo update drives each player's rating to the fixed point where their expected score against the matched near-rating pool is σ(0)=0.5. So at equilibrium EVERY player, regardless of true skill, has an expected win rate ~0.5, and the observed win rate is COMPRESSED almost flat across skill while skill is expressed as the equilibrium RATING, not a win-rate surplus. A same-seed two-regime control isolates the cause: run the SAME players with the SAME skills under RANDOM matching and their win rate becomes a near-perfect skill signal (top-vs-bottom skill-decile win-rate gap ~0.72, corr ~0.98); under SBMM the decile gap collapses to ~0.04 (~17× compression) even though the identical games' rating recovers skill with corr ~0.98 — matchmaking, not the skill model, turns win rate into a mirage; the skill signal is relocated out of the win rate into the rating. The residual corr(skill, SBMM-winrate) ~0.5 is a finite-population tail edge effect (extreme players match only inward), so the headline is the compressed decile GAP + the corr CONTRAST, not "win rate is uncorrelated with skill." GAME-domain (competitive matchmaking rating-system informational economics), deliberately distinct from prior game slots (P099 shop-reroll, P103 streak-shield, P107 energy-cap overflow, P091 gacha-pity, P095 rubber-band/PID).

## 💡 Session idea
"Match on the estimate, update the estimate to fit outcomes ⇒ the outcome column goes flat and stops encoding the latent quantity" is a reusable audit lens for any adversarial or control loop that equalizes its inputs. Whenever a controller/matchmaker pairs agents by their current skill estimate (SBMM), throttles by current load, or handicaps by current standing, the raw outcome (win/loss, success/fail, hit/miss) is driven toward a fixed point (~50%, ~target) and compressed almost flat across the latent quantity — so the outcome stops ranking skill/load/quality and the informative statistic becomes the latent ESTIMATE the loop is solving for (the rating), not the equalized outcome. The transferable audit: whenever a system equalizes opponents/inputs before measuring, ask "is the flat outcome the signature of a working equalizer, and is the latent estimate the real scoreboard?" — and grade by the estimate (rating, rating velocity), never by the equalized outcome. Candidate future game/fleet heads: auto-scaler CPU-utilization pinned at target (utilization stops signalling load — read the scaling signal), handicap/anti-tilt systems flattening score across skill, adaptive-difficulty (DDA) flattening completion rate across player skill, and A/B guardrail-metric clamping — each a "controller equalizes the input ⇒ the outcome flattens and the latent estimate carries the signal" claim with the same fixed-point compression + same-seed uncontrolled-regime control σ.
