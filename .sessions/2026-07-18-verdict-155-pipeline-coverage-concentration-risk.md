The head under verdict: the sales "pipeline coverage ratio" (expected pipeline value ÷ quota) is NOT a sufficient statistic for quota attainment. For bookings B = Σ Xᵢvᵢ with iid Bernoulli(p) win indicators Xᵢ over deals of value vᵢ (total V), E[B] = p·V and Var(B) = p(1−p)·Σvᵢ² = p(1−p)·V²·HHI, where HHI = Σwᵢ² is the Herfindahl concentration of deal-value shares wᵢ = vᵢ/V. Coverage E[B]/QUOTA fixes ONLY the first moment (p and V), so two pipelines with the same p and same V have identical coverage and identical expected bookings — yet at fixed p and V the variance RATIO equals the HHI RATIO exactly (p(1−p) and V² cancel), so a book concentrated in a few whales (high HHI) misses quota far more often than a granular book (HHI → 1/N floor) at the SAME coverage.

## 💡 Session idea

The verifier pins a SINGLE-HORIZON, POINT-IN-TIME world — one quota period, a fixed deal ladder, win/loss resolved once — and gates the miss-probability GAP between a granular and a concentrated book at a FROZEN coverage of 3.0. The load-bearing generalization the gates do NOT touch is the COVERAGE-BUFFER TRADE-OFF: in a real RevOps world an operator does not hold coverage fixed — they choose it, and the natural question is "how much MORE coverage does a concentrated book need to buy back the attainment a granular book gets for free?" A genuinely different next VENTURE slice pins that iso-attainment axis: hold p and the two HHI profiles fixed, then for each book SOLVE for the coverage C(HHI) that drives P(bookings < quota) down to a target miss rate α (say 10%), and pre-register (G-a) an iso-attainment-premium gate — the concentrated book requires a coverage premium ΔC = C(HHI_conc) − C(HHI_gran) that is strictly positive and ≥3σ (a whale book must carry materially MORE pipeline to hit the same attainment); (G-b) a premium-scaling gate — ΔC grows monotonically and ≥3σ with the HHI ratio (double the concentration, more than double the required buffer), converting the static "coverage is blind to HHI" into the actionable "concentration has a PRICE denominated in coverage points"; and (G-c) a diversification-vs-buffer budget control — one unit of HHI reduction (splitting the top whale into k equal deals) buys back attainment at a ≥3σ better rate than the equivalent coverage top-up does, proving the RevOps rule "de-concentrate the book before you pad the pipeline" is itself gate-able and directional. This turns V155's fixed-coverage miss-GAP into a coverage-PREMIUM curve a forecaster can act on — report not just the HHI but the coverage points it costs, and whether a dollar is better spent chasing more pipeline or breaking up the whales. A seed-pinnable iso-attainment / required-buffer mechanism distinct from this slice's frozen-coverage miss-gap and from the P138 revenue-CV concentration head.
