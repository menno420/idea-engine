Head: a venture fund's internal rate of return (IRR) is NOT monotone in the dollars it returns to limited partners. Because a bullet fund's IRR is moic^(1/T) − 1, a fund returning a small multiple quickly can out-IRR a fund returning a larger multiple slowly — so ranking funds on IRR can select the one that returns LESS money (1.3× in 18 months = 19.1% IRR beats 2.4× in 10 years = 9.2% IRR while returning roughly half the cash). The load-bearing companion: delaying the LP capital call with a subscription credit line shortens the IRR clock, so the reported IRR RISES even though LPs, net of line interest, receive FEWER dollars (MOIC falls). ## Probe questions
**1.** Is the IRR inversion real or an artifact of the bullet model? — A bullet fund's IRR is exactly moic^(1/T) − 1, so for fixed money returned a shorter horizon strictly raises IRR; G1 measures over 200k matched pairs that the fast-low-MOIC fund out-IRRs the slow-high-MOIC fund with a mean IRR gap > 0 at z≥3, and G3 re-derives the same sign under a staged (J-curve) multi-tranche distribution solved by bisection — not a bullet artifact.
**2.** Does the subscription line really raise IRR while LPs get fewer dollars? — G2 delays the LP capital call from t=0 to t=τ, funded by a credit line at rate c whose interest (1+c)^τ − 1 is netted out of the LP distribution; the LP MOIC strictly falls (mean ΔMOIC < 0 at z≥3) while the shorter IRR clock raises the reported IRR (mean ΔIRR > 0 at z≥3) — higher IRR, fewer dollars.
**3.** Is the speed premium distribution-free? — G3 replaces the bullet with staged two-year capital calls and spread early distributions under longer horizons and confirms the fast-fund IRR still exceeds the slow-fund IRR at z≥3.
**4.** Crossover, not the claim: the same time-value logic drives the annual-prepay implied-APR trap (P130) and any duration-vs-magnitude ranking (NPV-vs-IRR). Disclosed as a crossover, not asserted as the head? — disclosed; the verified head is the IRR speed trap for venture funds (rank-inversion on IRR + subscription-line inflation), scoped to unshaded MOIC-vs-IRR comparison.
