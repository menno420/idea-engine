## 💡 Session idea
Adding a free A→B shortcut to a congested 4-node routing network makes every selfishly-routed agent SLOWER: the shortcut is a dominant strategy that overloads the two congestible legs, lifting equilibrium latency from 1.5 to ~2.0. The harm is a monotone dose-response in the shortcut's own latency and vanishes above an interior threshold; a social-optimum router that minimizes TOTAL latency refuses the shortcut, proving the loss is a pure artifact of selfish routing, not of the added capacity. Decision-relevant to fleet task-routing: a fast shared lane under greedy lowest-latency dispatch can lower global throughput.
