# VERDICT 138 mirror — the coordination-overhead retrograde-throughput cliff (the Universal Scalability Law): naive linear scaling says a fleet of N agents on one coordinated job does N× the work, and the folk belief holds that adding agents at worst SATURATES (Amdahl) but never reverses. Both are wrong. A coordinated job pays two costs of different character — contention α (a serial/unparallelizable fraction; Amdahl caps speedup at 1/α, monotone) and coherency β (the cost of keeping agents consistent with each other, growing as the number of PAIRS ∝ N(N−1)). Adding the quadratic coherency term to Amdahl's denominator gives the Universal Scalability Law C(N)=N/(1+α(N−1)+βN(N−1)); because the denominator's coherency term grows as N² while the numerator grows as N, C(N) is NON-monotone — it rises, PEAKS at N*=√((1−α)/β), then DECLINES ("retrograde scaling"), so in completion-time form T(N)=(1−α)/N+(α−β)+βN the growing coherency term βN dominates past N* and every extra agent makes the fleet SLOWER. Two facts the "more agents = more throughput" instinct misses: (1) it is COHERENCY, not contention — with β=0 (Amdahl only) throughput is monotone up to the 1/α ceiling and never falls; the retrograde peak appears the instant β>0; (2) the optimum is FINITE, set by the α:β ratio — N*=√((1−α)/β)=31 on the pinned world, not "as many agents as you have." ## 💡 Session idea
This slice pins the USL retrograde cliff as a STATIC, HOMOGENEOUS-coherency law — one fixed β applied uniformly to every pair, so the coherency cost is exactly βN(N−1) and the optimum is the clean N*=√((1−α)/β). But the assumption that makes N* clean (every pair equally chatty) is exactly the one a real fleet violates: coordination is TOPOLOGICAL, not all-pairs — agents partitioned into shards gossip densely within a shard and sparsely across, so the effective pair-count is not N(N−1) but a block-structured sum. A genuinely different NEXT fleet slice, distinct from this all-pairs head and from the prior fleet closers (P101 winner's-curse, P105 delegation-loop collapse, P109 correlated-fleet variance floor, P113 two-choices routing cliff, P117 Young–Daly checkpoint, P121 hedged-request tail cure), pins a **coordination-topology β-reshaping** mechanism: partition N agents into K shards of N/K with intra-shard coherency β_in and inter-shard β_out ≪ β_in (hub-not-mesh gossip), so the effective coherency term becomes K·(N/K)(N/K−1)·β_in + (cross-shard pairs)·β_out and the optimum fleet size RISES with K (sharding buys headroom by lowering the effective β). Seed-pinnable head: sweep K over a grid at fixed total N and pre-register (G-a) an optimum-shift gate — N*(K) increases with K by ≥3σ (sharding raises the retrograde peak); (G-b) a β_out=β_in control — collapsing the inter-shard cost back to the intra-shard cost recovers this slice's single-β N*=31 within 3σ (isolating the TOPOLOGY, not the raw β, as the cause of the shift); (G-c) a throughput-at-fixed-N gate — at N=200 (past the single-β peak) the K-sharded fleet delivers strictly MORE throughput than the mesh fleet by ≥3σ, so the operator lesson sharpens from "size at N* and stop" to "to go faster past the peak, CUT coordination by partitioning state (raise K) — sharding, not more agents, is what moves the ceiling; only a genuinely β≈0 independent-subtask job scales with raw agent count, itself still Amdahl-capped at 1/α."
